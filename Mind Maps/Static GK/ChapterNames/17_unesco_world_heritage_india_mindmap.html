UNESCO World Heritage Sites in India

Cultural Sites · Natural Sites · Intangible Heritage · Creative Cities Network

Overview
  • As of July 2025, UNESCO has declared 44 sites in India as World Heritage Sites — 35 Cultural, 7 Natural, and 1 Mixed category.
  • Latest addition: Maratha Military Landscapes of India, added 11 July 2025 as the 44th site.
  • Kalka-Shimla Railway and Darjeeling Railway are UNESCO sites under India's Mountain Railways.

UNESCO Creative Cities Network (UCCN)
  • Started by UNESCO in 2009.
  • Objective: promotes cooperation among cities that recognise creativity as a strategic factor in urban development.
  • Aims to achieve Sustainable Development Goals through innovative thinking and implementation.
  • Covers seven creative fields: music, arts, folk crafts, design, cinema, literature, digital arts, and culinary arts.
