  • This chapter compiles major Government of India & RBI committees frequently asked as "committee-recommendation" matching questions in BPSC/BSSC.
  • Grouped thematically: Banking Reform, Taxation & Fiscal Reforms, Social Sector & Poverty, Agriculture & Rural Economy, Monetary Policy & Inflation, Capital Market & Investments, and Financial Inclusion & Digital.
  • Focus on matching the committee name → chairperson-linked scheme/recommendation, since dates and names are commonly swapped in MCQs.
⚡ QUICK RECALL Many committees share chairpersons across years — e.g. Urjit Patel (2013) appears under both Banking Reform & Monetary Policy for the CPI-based monetary policy framework.
🏦 Banking Reform Committees
CommitteePurpose / Objective
Goiporia (1990)Improved customer service — led to Banking Ombudsman Scheme (1995)
Narasimhan Committee I (1991)4-tier banking structure, quasi-autonomous supervisory body, asset classification, Asset Reconstruction Fund
Narasimhan Committee II (1998)Bank mergers for strength, narrow banking for weak banks, review of banking laws
Khan (2005)Financial inclusion & Lead Bank Scheme
Damodaran (2010)Customer service and grievance redressal mechanisms
Usha Thorat (2011)Higher capital norms (12%) for NBFCs, asset classification and provisioning
MV Nair (2011)Priority sector lending guidelines
Urjit Patel (2013)CPI-based monetary policy framework
Bimal Jalan (2013)Recommended banking licenses for Bandhan Microfinance and IDFC
PJ Nayak (2014)Repeal Bank Nationalisation Act & SBI Acts, set up Bank Investment Company (BIC)
Arun Jaitley (2017)Merger of public sector banks
⚠ EXAM TRAP Narasimhan I (1991) recommended a 4-tier banking structure; Narasimhan II (1998) focused on bank mergers & narrow banking — exams frequently swap which committee did what.
💰 Taxation & Fiscal Reforms Committees
CommitteePurpose / Objective
Raja Chelliah (1991)Comprehensive tax reforms
Rekhi (1991)Indirect tax reforms
L. K. Jha (1997)Indirect tax reforms
Rakesh Mohan (2001)Financial sector & infrastructure reforms

⚠ EXAM TRAP Both Rekhi (1991) and L.K. Jha (1997) worked on indirect tax reforms — six years apart. Don't confuse which year belongs to which committee.
👥 Social Sector & Poverty Committees
CommitteePurpose / Objective
Bhagwati (1973)Unemployment estimation
YK Alagh (1979)Formula to measure poverty (based on calorie consumption)
DT Lakdawala (1993)State-specific poverty line (CPI-AL for Rural and CPI-IW for Urban)
Prof. C. P. Chandrashekar (2007)Construction of service price index
Tendulkar (2009)Shifted calorie-based poverty line to nutrition-based poverty basket
Rangarajan (2014)Revised poverty base year (2011–12)
⚡ QUICK RECALL Poverty-line evolution: YK Alagh (calorie formula) → Lakdawala (state-specific CPI) → Tendulkar (nutrition basket) → Rangarajan (2011–12 base year revision).

📈 Monetary Policy & Inflation Committees
CommitteePurpose / Objective
Chakravarti I (1985)Review of monetary system
Chakravarti II (1985)Reconsideration of monetary system
S. S. Tarapore (I) (1997)Capital account convertibility
S. S. Tarapore (II) (2007)Second review of capital account convertibility
Urjit Patel (2013)CPI-based monetary policy framework
⚠ EXAM TRAP Tarapore I (1997) and Tarapore II (2007) — both by the same person, ten years apart, both on Capital Account Convertibility. Exam may ask "which came first" or "which year".
