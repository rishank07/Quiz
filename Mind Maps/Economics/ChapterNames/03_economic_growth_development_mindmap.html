Economic Growth and Development

Growth vs Development · HDI, PQLI, NEW, PPP, GNH · Measurement Indices — BPSC / BSSC

Economic Growth
  • Related to continuous increase in Gross Domestic Product (GDP), Gross National Product (GNP), and Per Capita Income (PCI)
  • Related to quantitative change
  • Most appropriate measure of economic growth: Real Per Capita Income
Per Capita Income = National Income ÷ Total Population
Economic Development
  • Efficient utilization of all factors of production
  • With continuous and long-term growth in national income and per capita income, there is improvement in people's standard of living
  • Related to both quantitative and qualitative change
  • Depends on Natural Resources, Capital Formation, and Size of Market
Prof. Amartya Sen's DefinitionEmpowerment and expansion of capabilities
Amartya Sen — Nobel Prize1998

Environment Kuznets Curve: Shows the relationship between environmental quality and economic development (Per Capita Income).

⚡ QUICK RECALL
Growth = quantitative only; Development = quantitative + qualitative — the single biggest exam distinction in this topic.

Related ToUnited Nations Development Programme (UNDP)
Published Since1990, by UNDP
IndicatorsLife Expectancy at Birth, Education, Per Capita Income

⚠ EXAM TRAP
Don't confuse HDI's score scale (0 to 1) with PQLI's scale (1 to 100) — a very common mix-up in options.

Decade1970s

The concept implies that sustainable development should take a holistic approach towards national progress, giving equal importance to non-economic aspects of well-being and happiness.

GNH = Bhutan's original contribution to development economics — 33 indicators across 9 domains, prioritizing happiness over pure GDP.
Economic Development vs Economic Growth
Economic DevelopmentEconomic Growth
Qualitative ConceptQuantitative Concept
Along with increase in production, technological and institutional changes are also importantA steady process; does not involve any kind of innovation
Requires structural transformationStructural change already completed; only production can be increased
Result of creative forces generated through proper control, direction, and guidanceOutcome of planned processes
Involves social, cultural, economic, qualitative, and quantitative changesRelated only to the increase in production
⚠ EXAM TRAP
Growth is a subset within Development — every instance of development involves some growth, but growth alone (without qualitative change) does not amount to development.
