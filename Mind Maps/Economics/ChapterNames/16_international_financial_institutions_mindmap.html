Thematic Categories
  • This chapter covers major International Financial & Economic Institutions frequently tested in BPSC/BSSC — establishment year, headquarters, membership numbers and objectives.
  • Grouped thematically: Bretton Woods Twins (IMF, World Bank), Trade Bodies (WTO, GATT, NAFTA, APEC), Development Banks (ADB, AIIB, NDB), Regional Groupings (ASEAN, SAARC, BIMSTEC), Global Forums (WEF, BRICS, G-20, G-7, EU), and Other Bodies (OPEC, UNCTAD).
  • Special focus on tricky numbers — member counts, exact establishment dates, and recent changes (new members, exits) which are high-yield for Prelims.
⚡ QUICK RECALL IMF + World Bank = the two "Bretton Woods Institutions", both established in 1944 at the Bretton Woods Conference (USA).
🏦 International Monetary Fund (IMF)
Established1944
HeadquartersWashington D.C.
Member Countries191 (191st: Liechtenstein)
India joined27th December, 1945

Objectives

  • To promote international monetary cooperation.
  • Correction of adverse balance of payments of member countries.
  • Provides short-term loan assistance to member countries.

Special Drawing Rights (SDR) — "Paper Gold"

  • SDR value is based on a basket of five currencies:
  • US Dollar
  • Euro
  • Japanese Yen
  • Pound Sterling
  • Chinese Renminbi (Yuan)
⚠ EXAM TRAP SDR is nicknamed "Paper Gold" — don't confuse it with actual currency; it is an international reserve asset, not a physical currency.

🌐 World Trade Organisation (WTO)
Established1st January, 1995 (replacing GATT)
HeadquartersGeneva
Member Countries166 (166th: Timor-Leste)
ObjectiveRegulation & promotion of international trade
⚠ EXAM TRAP GATT (General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade) was established 1st January 1948 — WTO replaced it decades later on 1st January 1995. Don't mix up the two dates.

🌏 ASEAN

Full Form: Association of Southeast Asian Nations

Established8th December, 1985
HeadquartersJakarta (Indonesia)
Members10
  • Indonesia, Philippines, Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand, Cambodia, Vietnam, Myanmar, Brunei and Laos.
🌏 SAARC

Full Form: South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation

Established8th December, 1985
HeadquartersKathmandu (Nepal)
Members8
  • India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan, Sri Lanka, Maldives and Afghanistan.
⚠ EXAM TRAP ASEAN and SAARC were both established on the exact same date — 8th December 1985 — but are entirely different groupings (Southeast Asia vs South Asia). Frequently confused in exams.
🌏 BIMSTEC

Full Form: Bay of Bengal Initiative for Multi-Sectoral Technical and Economic Cooperation

Established1997
Members7
  • Bangladesh, India, Sri Lanka, Thailand, Nepal, Myanmar and Bhutan.
🌍 World Economic Forum (WEF)
EstablishedJanuary 1971 (by Klaus Schwab)
HeadquartersGeneva
Annual MeetingDavos (January)
ObjectiveShape global, regional & industrial agendas responsibly
🌍 BRICS
Established2009
First Summit2009, Yekaterinburg (Russia)
Current Members11 (11th: Indonesia, joined Jan 2025)
South Africa joined2010
17th BRICS Summit6–7 July 2025, Rio de Janeiro (Brazil)
  • Five new members added on 1st January 2024: Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Ethiopia, UAE, Iran.
  • The word 'BRIC' was first used in 2001 by Jim O'Neill of Goldman Sachs.
⚡ QUICK RECALL BRICS growth timeline: 2001 term coined → 2009 first summit (4 nations) → 2010 South Africa joins (BRICS) → 2024 five new members → 2025 Indonesia joins (11 total).
🌍 G-20
Established1999 (Berlin)
Members19 countries + European Union + African Union
NatureOrganism of Industrial & Developing Nations
🌍 G-7
Established1975
Members7 — USA, France, Britain, Italy, Germany, Canada, Japan
  • Russia joined in 1997 (forming G-8) but was removed in 2014.
🌍 European Union (EU)
Established1957 (as European Economic Community)
Became EU1993
Members27 (England exited in 2020 — Brexit)
HeadquartersBrussels (Belgium)
Common CurrencyEuro
Eurozone Countries20 (adopting Euro)
⚠ EXAM TRAP EU has 27 members but only 20 use the Euro — total EU members ≠ Eurozone members. A common numeric trap.

Full Form: Organisation of Petroleum Exporting Countries

HeadquartersVienna (Austria)
ObjectiveCoordinate petroleum policies among member countries
  • Angola withdrew from OPEC on 1st January, 2024.
