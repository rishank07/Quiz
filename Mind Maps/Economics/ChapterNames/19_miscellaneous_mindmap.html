  • This chapter compiles miscellaneous but high-yield static GK topics: Indian certification/quality marks, global reports & indices with their issuing institutions, and foundational economics books with authors & publication years.

✅ Certification Marks in India
MarkIntroducedKey Details
ISI Mark (Indian Standards Institution)1947Renamed to BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) in 1987 | For industrial products
Agmark (Agricultural Produce Mark)1937Mandatory for agricultural products | Made compulsory by law in 1986
FPO Mark (Fruit Products Order)1955For processed fruit products of standardized quality | Compulsory since 2006
Eco Mark1991 (by BIS)For pollution-free / environment-friendly products
BS Mark (Bharat Stage Mark)2000For vehicles manufactured per pollution control standards
BIS Hallmark2000 (gold) / 2005 (silver)Issued by BIS for gold and silver jewellery
India Organic Mark2002Under National Standards for Organic Production | For organic agricultural produce
Silk Mark2004Issued by Silk Mark Organisation of India | For pure/genuine silk garments
⚠ EXAM TRAP Agmark (1937) is actually older than ISI Mark (1947) — despite ISI being more commonly known, Agmark was introduced a decade earlier.
⚡ QUICK RECALL ISI → renamed BIS (1987); BIS itself later issues both the Hallmark (gold/silver) and Eco Mark — BIS is the common thread linking multiple certification marks.
