📖 Council of Ministers — Structure
Cabinet Ministers
Senior rank · Attend Cabinet meetings · Full portfolio
Ministers of State
Independent or attached to Cabinet minister
Deputy Ministers
Assist Cabinet / MoS
  • Aid & advice to President — not binding first time, binding second time President can return advice once; on re-submission it becomes binding
  • President cannot act without Cabinet advice → as per 44th Amendment
  • Council is responsible to Lok Sabha
  • Collective resignation after losing confidence of Lok Sabha
⚠ Exam Trap

Council of Ministers ka advice President ke liye pehli baar binding nahi, lekin doosri baar binding hai (44th Amendment, 1978). Isse pehle President ko absolute discretion tha.

  • Must be a Citizen of India
  • Age: 25+ for Lok Sabha · 30+ for Rajya Sabha
  • Must be an MP — if not, must become within 6 months
  • Max Ministers = 15% of total Lok Sabha strength (incl. PM) 91st Constitutional Amendment
  • PM appointed by President; other ministers by President on PM's advice
  • PM resigns to President
  • Salaries paid from Consolidated Fund of India Art 75(6)
  • Term → 5 years or until Lok Sabha dissolved
⚠ Exam Trap

91st Amendment — maximum 15% MPs se zyada ministers nahi ho sakte. Iska matlab: Lok Sabha 543 members → max ~81 ministers (including PM). Anti-defection bhi is amendment ka hissa hai.

👑 Prime Minister's Role
  • Head of the Council of Ministers
  • Leader of the House (Lok Sabha)
  • Key figure in Parliamentary Democracy
  • Real Executive — President is nominal; PM is actual
  • Link between President and Cabinet Art. 78 — PM's duty to inform President about Cabinet decisions
💡 Quick Recall

Art. 78 — PM ka duty hai ki woh President ko Cabinet decisions ke baare mein inform kare aur President jo bhi information maange woh de. Ye ek important constitutional obligation hai.

⚖️ Attorney General of India
  • Part V · Article 76
  • Chief Legal Advisor of Government of India
  • Law Officer of India (highest law officer)
  • Appointed by President under Art. 76(1)
  • Must be qualified to be appointed Judge of Supreme Court
  • Can participate in proceedings of both Houses But has NO right to vote
  • Not disqualified from private legal practice
  • Salary & service conditions fixed by the Government
⚠ Exam Trap

Attorney General Parliament ke dono Houses mein participate kar sakta hai lekin vote nahi de sakta. Aur woh private practice bhi kar sakta hai — ye judges se alag hai.

🏛️ Notable AGs
  • First Attorney General → M.C. Setalvad
  • Current Attorney General → R. Venkataramani
💡 Quick Recall

Attorney General (Centre) = Art. 76
Advocate General (State) = Art. 165
Centre ka AG, State ka Advocate General ke equivalent hai. +89 Rule se yaad karo!

⚠ Exam Trap

Attorney General & Solicitor General alag hain. AG = Constitutional post (Art. 76). Solicitor General = Government appointee, no constitutional basis. AG sabse senior hai.

🔢 +89 Rule — Centre to State Equivalents
The Magic Formula
Centre Article + 89 = State Equivalent Article
Centre Article +89 Article State
PM (Council of Ministers) 74 → 163 CM (Council of Ministers)
Presidential Pardon 72 → 161 Governor's Pardon
Attorney General 76 → 165 Advocate General
Parliament 79 → 168 State Legislature
Rajya Sabha 80 → 169 Legislative Council
Lok Sabha 81 → 170 Legislative Assembly
💡 Quick Recall

Ye trick exam mein article number bhool jaane par kaam aati hai. Centre ka article number + 89 = State equivalent. Sirf kuch exceptions hain — baaki sab isi rule mein aate hain.

⚠ Exam Trap

+89 Rule ek memory trick hai, constitutional rule nahi. Iska use sirf article numbers yaad karne ke liye karo. Exam mein isko "constitutional provision" mat bolo!

💡 Quick Recall

Art. 74 — Cabinet ke advice President pe binding hai (44th Amendment se). Art. 75(2) — Ministers President ki "pleasure" pe hote hain, lekin practically PM ke kehne pe hata diye jaate hain. Art. 78 — PM ka duty hai President ko inform karna.
