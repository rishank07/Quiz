⚠ Exam Trap — Amendment-wise Additions

21st Amendment (1967) → Sindhi (1 language). 71st Amendment (1992) → Konkani, Manipuri, Nepali (3 languages). 92nd Amendment (2003) → Bodo, Dogri, Santhali, Maithili (4 languages). Total added = 1+3+4 = 8. Original 14 + 8 = 22.

⚠ Exam Trap — BPSC Special

Maithili — Bihar ki major language — 92nd Amendment (2003) se 8th Schedule mein add hui. Santhali bhi 92nd Amendment se add hui. Bihar se related language question aata hai!

💡 Quick Recall

English is NOT in 8th Schedule. Sanskrit hai. Kashmiri, Sindhi — koi specific region nahi (state/UT without exclusive territory). Agar koi language 8th Schedule mein hai toh uske speaker UPSC exam Hindi/English ke alawa us language mein de sakte hain.

🏛️ Classical Languages — 11 Total

  • Based on recommendations from committees formed by Sahitya Akademi
  • NOT mentioned in the Constitution
Criteria for Classical Language Status
  • Language must be 1500–2000 years old
  • Related to Traditional Literature
  • Availability of Ancient Texts & Literature

⚠ Exam Trap — Hindi Diwas vs World Hindi Day

Hindi Diwas = 14 September (1949) — National level, India mein manaya jaata hai. World Hindi Day (Vishwa Hindi Diwas) = 10 January — International level, globally celebrated. Dono alag dates hain — confuse mat karo!

💡 Quick Recall — Must-Know

Art. 343 — Hindi = Union Official Language. Art. 348 — SC & HC proceedings in English (unless President orders otherwise). Art. 351 — Centre's duty to develop Hindi. 8th Schedule — 22 languages (NOT part of Art. 343–351 range but linked).

⚠ Exam Trap

Art. 348 — SC and HC language = English (unless President authorizes otherwise). Many assume courts work in Hindi — but constitutionally, English is the court language at the highest levels.
