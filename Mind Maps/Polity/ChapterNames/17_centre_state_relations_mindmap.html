💡 Quick Recall — Conflict Rule

If Parliament + State Legislature dono Concurrent list pe law banate hain → Parliament ka law prevails (Art. 254). Exception: State law got President's assent → State law prevails in that state.

⚠️ When Parliament Can Legislate on State List
  • National Interest Art 249 2/3rd majority in Rajya Sabha — resolution passed
  • Agreement between States Two or more states request Parliament
  • International Treaties & Agreements Art 253 To implement international obligations
  • During National Emergency Art 352 Parliament gets power to legislate on entire State List
  • During President's Rule Art 356 Parliament acts as State Legislature
⚠ Exam Trap

Art. 249 — National Interest mein Parliament State List pe law bana sakti hai lekin sirf 1 year ke liye valid, renewable hai. RS ka 2/3rd majority zaroori hai — LS ki majority kaafi nahi!

💡 Quick Recall — Territorial Jurisdiction

Parliament — Entire country + extra-territorial laws (Art. 245). State Legislature — Only within State boundaries.

🏢 Administrative Relations (Art. 256–263)
  • Art 256 Centre can give directions to States To ensure compliance with central laws
  • Art 257 Control of Union over States in certain cases States cannot impede Union's executive power
  • Mutual delegation of functions To avoid rigidity & deadlock in governance
⚠ Exam Trap

Art. 256 — Centre State ko direction de sakta hai ki central laws follow karo. Agar State nahi maani toh Art. 356 (President's Rule) lag sakta hai.

👮 All India Services (Joint Control)
  • Full Control (Centre): ✦ Appointments ✦ Disciplinary Actions ✦ Promotion & Training
  • Immediate Control (State): ✦ Suspension Power
💡 Quick Recall

All India Services = IAS, IPS, IFoS (Indian Forest Service). These officers serve both Centre and State — dual control. Appointment (UPSC exam) → Centre. Day-to-day posting → State.

⚠ Exam Trap

State sirf suspend kar sakta hai All India Service officer ko — dismiss, remove ya compulsory retire NAHI kar sakta. Ye power sirf Centre (DoPT) ke paas hai.

💰 Financial Relations (Art. 268–293)
  • Art 268 Duties levied by Union, collected by States Example: Stamp Duty on Bills of Exchange & Promissory Notes
  • UTs mein → Collected by Union But NOT deposited into CF of India
  • Art 269A Tax distribution between Union & States
  • Art 280 Finance Commission
⚠ Exam Trap

Art. 268 — Stamp duty pe Union ka tax levy karta hai lekin States collect karte hain aur apne paas rakhte hain. Ye centre-state financial cooperation ka example hai.

💡 Quick Recall — GST Council

GST Council — Art. 279A (101st Amendment, 2016). Centre-State financial coordination ka most recent mechanism. Chairman = Union Finance Minister.

Finance Commission quasi-judicial body hai — it's not a judicial body and not a pure executive body. Its recommendations are advisory — not binding on government (though usually accepted).

✅ Qualifications — Finance Commission
  • Qualifications → As determined by the President
  • Other Members must include: ✦ One with qualification equivalent to High Court Judge ✦ Experts in finance & accounts
Functions of Finance Commission
  • Distribution of taxes between Centre & States
  • Grants-in-aid from CF of India to States
  • Advice on matters referred by President → not binding
  • Recommend measures to augment CF of States To supplement resources of Panchayats & Municipalities
🤝 Inter-State Council (Art. 263)
  • Established by President Art 263
  • Recommended by Sarkaria Commission (1983–87)
  • Established in 1990 under PM V.P. Singh (Janata Dal)
  • Reconstituted on 19th May, 2022 to promote Cooperative Federalism
  • Meetings → At least 3 times a year
Key Functions
  • Resolves Centre-State Disputes
  • Investigates Inter-State Disputes
  • Coordinates policies and implementation
⚠ Exam Trap

Sarkaria Commission = 1983 (established). Inter-State Council = 1990 (established). Recommend karna 1983 mein, establish karna 1990 mein. Ye difference poochha jaata hai!

🗺️ Zonal Councils
Key Facts
Formed under States Reorganization Act, 1956 · Chairman = Union Home Minister · 5 Zones + 1 NE Council
Purpose
  • Promote dialogue among states + Resolve mutual disputes
  • Avoid regionalism, linguistic chauvinism & particularism
  • Support implementation of schemes
  • Ensure Political Unity
  • Exchange of ideas and experiences
Zone HQ States / UTs Covered
🔵 Northern New Delhi J&K, Ladakh, Himachal Pradesh, Haryana, Rajasthan, Delhi, Chandigarh
🟠 Central Prayagraj Uttarakhand, Madhya Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, Chhattisgarh
🟢 Eastern Kolkata Bihar, Jharkhand, Odisha, West Bengal
🟣 Western Mumbai Gujarat, Maharashtra, Goa, Dadra & Nagar Haveli, Daman & Diu
🔴 Southern Chennai Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Telangana, Puducherry
💡 Quick Recall — For BPSC

Bihar → Eastern Zonal Council — HQ Kolkata. Eastern zone mein: Bihar, Jharkhand, Odisha, West Bengal. Bihar BPSC ke liye ye yaad karna zaroori hai!

🌿 North-Eastern Council
  • Established 1972 under North-Eastern Council Act, 1971
  • Initially 7 states: Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram
  • Currently 8 states → Sikkim added in 2002
  • Members → 1 Chairman + 3 Other Members + 8 CMs & Governors
⚠ Exam Trap

Zonal Councils = 5 zones (Northern, Central, Eastern, Western, Southern). North-East = Separate NE Council (8 states, est. 1972). NE Council is NOT one of the 5 zonal councils!

🌊 Inter-State River Water Disputes
  • Constitutional Provision → Art 262
  • Statutory Basis → Inter-State River Water Disputes Act, 1956 (amended 2017)
  • Composition → 1 Chairperson + 1 Vice Chairperson + Max 6 members
  • Term → 5 years for Chairperson · VP & members till dispute resolved

⚠ Exam Trap

Art. 262 — Parliament can by law exclude SC's jurisdiction on inter-state river disputes. Ye special provision hai — normally SC har cheez pe jurisdiction rakhta hai, lekin river disputes mein Parliament SC ko bar kar sakti hai!

💡 Quick Recall

1969 mein ek saath 3 tribunals bane — Krishna, Godavari, Narmada. Cauvery dispute sabse famous/controversial raha (Karnataka vs Tamil Nadu). Mahanadi (2018) — sabse recent.
