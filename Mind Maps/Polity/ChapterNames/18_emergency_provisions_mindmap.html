⚠ Exam Trap

1975 Emergency mein President = Fakhruddin Ali Ahmed. PM = Indira Gandhi. Grounds = "Internal Disturbance" (baad mein 44th Amendment ne replace kiya with "Armed Rebellion"). 1975 Emergency = darkest chapter of Indian democracy.

🔴 National Emergency (Art. 352)
  • Grounds: War / External Aggression / Armed Rebellion "Internal Disturbance" replaced by "Armed Rebellion" — 44th Amendment (1978)
  • Declaration: On written recommendation of the Cabinet President alone cannot declare — Cabinet recommendation mandatory
  • Approval: Special Majority — within 1 month (both Houses) If LS dissolved → within 30 days of 1st sitting of new LS
  • Extension: Parliament's approval required every 6 months 44th Amendment introduced this safeguard
  • Duration: Unlimited — as long as Parliament keeps approving
  • Revocation: By Simple Majority of Lok Sabha Special resolution — majority of all members present & voting
⚠ Exam Trap — Majority Confusion

Approval of National Emergency = Special Majority (2/3rd + majority of total membership). Revocation = Simple Majority of LS. Different majorities for passing vs revoking — classic MCQ trap!

🔍 Effects of National Emergency
⛔ Negative Effects
  • Art. 19 (6 freedoms) suspended
  • Centre can legislate on State List
  • President directs State executives
  • LS term extended by 1 year
  • Revenue sharing altered
✅ What STAYS Protected
  • Art. 20 — Protection from conviction
  • Art. 21 — Right to Life (NEVER suspended)
  • Writ of Habeas Corpus (post 44th Amend.)
  • Federal structure (partly)
💡 Quick Recall

Minerva Mills Case (1980) — Emergency subject to Judicial Review. SC can review whether grounds for Emergency were valid. Courts are NOT completely shut out during Emergency.

💡 Quick Recall — Art. 355

Art. 355 — Duty of Union to protect states from external aggression & internal disturbance, and to ensure governance in accordance with Constitution.

⚠ Exam Trap

National Emergency approval = Special Majority. President's Rule approval = Simple Majority. Revocation of President's Rule = President anytime (no Parliament approval needed). Teen alag-alag rules hain!

⚖️ Effects of President's Rule
  • Division of Power — Abolished (Centre takes over)
  • State Council of Ministers → Dissolved / Suspended
  • Legislative Assembly → Suspended or Dissolved
  • Fundamental Rights → NO effect (FRs not suspended)
  • Laws made during President's Rule → Remain valid for 6 months after revocation
Key Cases
  • S.R. Bommai Case (1994) — President's Rule subject to Judicial Review Most important case — SC can review validity of Art. 356 proclamation
  • K.K. Babu v. Union of India — Centre's power to give directions to States
⚠ Exam Trap — S.R. Bommai Case

S.R. Bommai (1994) — biggest judgment on Art. 356. SC ne kaha President's Rule arbitrary tarike se nahi laga sakte. Floor test zaroori hai pehle. President's Rule ek last resort hai, pehla option nahi.

Key Provisions During Financial Emergency
  • Suspension of Union-State tax distribution
  • Power to reduce salaries & allowances of Union & State employees Including salaries of SC & HC Judges
  • President can direct States on financial matters
  • Money Bills of States can be reserved for President's consideration
⚠ Exam Trap

Financial Emergency mein SC & HC judges ki salary bhi reduce ki ja sakti hai — ye normally constitutional guarantee ke against hai (Art. 125). Emergency mein ye exception hai. Financial Emergency kabhi laga nahi — ye theoretical provision hai.

⚖️ Effect on Fundamental Rights — Art. 358 & 359
Art. 358
  • Only Art. 19 suspended (6 freedoms)
  • Operates ONLY during war & external aggression
  • NOT during armed rebellion
  • Applicable throughout the country
  • Automatic — no separate order needed
Art. 359
  • All FRs (except Art. 20 & 21) may be suspended
  • Both war/external aggression AND armed rebellion
  • May apply to entire country or part
  • Requires Presidential Order
  • Not automatic — needs specific order
🛡️ NEVER SUSPENDED — Even During Emergency
Art. 20
Protection from Conviction
Art. 21
Right to Life & Liberty
⚠ Exam Trap — Art. 358 vs 359

Art. 358 = Automatic, only Art. 19, only war/external aggression. Art. 359 = Presidential Order needed, all FR except 20 & 21, both types of emergency. Dono mein main difference — Armed Rebellion mein Art. 358 apply nahi hota!

💡 Quick Recall

44th Amendment (1978) — Art. 20 & 21 ko explicitly emergency mein bhi protect kiya. Pehle 1975 emergency mein Habeas Corpus bhi suspend ho gaya tha (ADM Jabalpur case). Isliye ye protection add ki gayi.

⚠ Exam Trap — 3 Key Differences

1. Approval: National = Special Majority · Others = Simple Majority. 2. Deadline: National = 1 month · Others = 2 months. 3. Revocation: National = LS Simple Majority · President's Rule & Financial = President anytime.

⚠ Exam Trap — Ambedkar Quotes

💡 Quick Recall — 44th Amendment Safeguards

"Armed Rebellion" (not "Internal Disturbance") · Cabinet's written recommendation mandatory · LS can revoke by Simple Majority · Art. 20 & 21 explicitly protected · 6-month renewal required. Ye sab 44th Amendment (1978) ke safeguards hain jo 1975 Emergency ke baad aaye!
