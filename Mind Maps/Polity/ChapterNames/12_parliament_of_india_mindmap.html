🚫 Disqualification (Art. 102) & Defection
  • Unsound Mind · Insolvent
  • Imprisonment of more than 2 years
  • Absent for 60+ days without permission
  • Defection → 52nd Amendment (1985) → 10th Schedule
Conditions for Defection:
  • Independent member joining a party
  • Nominated member joining a party after 6 months
  • Not obeying whip
  • Member joining another party Exception: 2/3rd members joining (party merger)
💡 Quick Recall

Kihoto Hollohan v. Zachillhu (1992) — Presiding Officer decision final, but Judiciary can review. Disqualification on defection decided by Speaker (LS) / Chairman (RS).

🛡️ Privileges of Members
  • No criminal liability for speech in Parliament
  • No arrest in Civil Cases — 40 days before & after session
  • Arrest in Criminal cases is allowed
⚠ Exam Trap

Privilege sirf civil cases mein milti hai — criminal cases mein MP/MLA ko arrest kiya ja sakta hai. Aur ye protection 40 days before + session + 40 days after tak hai.

💡 Quick Recall

Salaries & Allowances → Parliament dwara decide · Paid from Consolidated Fund of India · Oath under 3rd Schedule.

📏 Delimitation
  • After each census — Delimitation of LS and State Assembly seats
  • 1st Delimitation Commission → 1952
  • Till now constituted 4 times: 1952, 1963, 1973, 2002
  • Delimitation Commission Act: 1962, 1972, 2002
  • 84th Amendment (2001) → No change in seats till 2026
⚠ Exam Trap

84th Amendment ne freeze kar diya seats 2026 tak. 2026 ke baad census ke baad delimitation hoga. Ye Southern states ke baare mein controversy ka point bhi hai.

🎙️ Speaker of Lok Sabha
  • 1 Speaker + 1 Deputy Speaker — elected from LS members
  • Chairs Joint Sitting of both Houses (Art. 108) — called by President If Speaker absent → Deputy Speaker → Deputy Chairman of RS
  • Appoints members to Parliamentary Committees
  • Casting Vote in case of tie
  • Adjournment of meetings
  • Resignation: Speaker gives to Deputy Speaker & vice versa
  • Removal: Majority of then members · 14 days prior notice

⚠ Exam Trap

Pro-tem Speaker — Senior-most MP after LS election · Appointed by President · Only job: administer oath to new MPs & conduct Speaker election.

🪑 Chairman & Deputy Chairman of Rajya Sabha
  • Chairman → Vice President of India Art 89(1)
  • VP is NOT a member of any House
  • Deputy Chairman Resignation → to Chairman
  • Deputy Chairman Removal → Majority of Rajya Sabha
  • If both Chairman & Deputy Chairman absent → one from panel of 10 (appointed by President) presides
  • During pending removal → Chairman/Deputy Chairman shall not preside
⚠ Exam Trap

VP Rajya Sabha ka member nahi hota — sirf Chairman hota hai. Isliye VP Parliament ke kisi bhi house mein vote nahi de sakta (unless as Chairman in RS — casting vote in tie).

💡 Quick Recall

Joint Sitting hierarchy: Speaker (LS) → Deputy Speaker (LS) → Deputy Chairman (RS). Joint sitting mein LS wale preside karte hain, RS wale nahi.

⚠ Exam Trap

Summoning (session bulana) = President ka kaam. Adjournment (session rokna temporarily) = Speaker ka kaam. Ye dono alag hain — commonly confuse hota hai!

⏰ Parliamentary Time — Question & Zero Hour
  • Question Hour → 11 AM – 12 PM
  • Zero Hour → 12 PM – 1 PM
💡 Quick Recall

Zero Hour — Constitution mein mentioned NAHI hai. Ye ek informal practice hai. Question Hour mein starred/unstarred/short notice questions aate hain.

  • Special Address of President → Motion of Thanks required ✦ On new Lok Sabha formation ✦ First session of each New Year
  • Power of President → Address both Houses · Send messages
  • Parliamentary Bill (Art. 107) → Introduced in either House
📜 Major Types of Bills
Ordinary Bill
  • Public Bill: Any House · 7 days notice · Joint Sitting possible
  • Private Member Bill: Non-minister introduces · 30 days notice
Money Bill (Art. 110)
  • Introduced in LS only with prior consent of President
  • Money Bill or not → decided by LS Speaker
  • RS can withhold for 14 days (no amendment power)
  • No Joint Sitting provision
Constitution Amendment Bill
  • No Joint Sitting provision
  • No veto power to President → 24th Amendment, 1971
⚠ Exam Trap

Money Bill mein RS ka role sirf 14 days suggest karna hai — LS accept/reject kar sakti hai. All Money Bills are Financial Bills but NOT vice versa. 3 Joint Sittings abhi tak: Dowry (1961), Banking (1977), POTA (2002).

✋ Types of Veto by President
🔴 Absolute Veto
Withhold assent completely — bill lapse ho jaata hai
🔵 Suspensive Veto
Send back for reconsideration — Parliament can override
🟠 Pocket Veto
No action taken — bill pending indefinitely
⬜ Qualified Veto
NOT available to Indian President (USA mein hota hai)
💡 Quick Recall

Pocket Veto — Giani Zail Singh ne India Post Office (Amendment) Bill pe use kiya. President ne na assent diya, na return kiya — bill pending raha.

⚠ Exam Trap

Constitution Amendment Bill pe President veto nahi kar sakta (24th Amendment, 1971). Ordinary Bill pe Absolute/Suspensive/Pocket veto possible. Qualified Veto India mein NAHI hai.

💰 Cut Motions (Appropriation Bill)
  • Policy Cut Motion → Amount of demand reduced to ₹1/-
  • Economy Cut Motion → Amount reduced by specified amount
  • Token Cut Motion → Amount reduced by ₹100/-
⚠ Exam Trap

Policy Cut = ₹1 · Token Cut = ₹100. Ye ulta lag sakta hai — Policy cut mein zyada symbolism hai (government ki policy se disagreement), Token cut mein specific grievance express hoti hai.

⚡ Major Parliamentary Motions
1. Original Motion
Completely independent — not dependent on any other motion Examples: Adjournment Motion, Motion of Thanks
2. No-Confidence Motion
Only in Lok Sabha · Requires min. 50 members as proposers · No reason required · If passed → Government resigns 🏛️ 1st No-Confidence Motion: 1963 by J.B. Kriplani against Nehru (not passed) · Total 27 till now · 15 against Indira Gandhi!
3. Confidence Motion
By ruling party on direction of President — to prove majority in Lok Sabha
4. Censure Motion
Against one minister or CoM · No permission needed · No reason required · Government not bound to resign (but humiliating) Unlike No-Confidence Motion — Censure doesn't bring down the government
5. Adjournment Motion (Work Stoppage)
Draws attention to urgent public importance issue · Needs support of at least 50 members · Government doesn't resign
⚠ Exam Trap

No-Confidence = Government girna (sirf LS mein) · Censure = Embarrass karna (government nahi girti) · Adjournment = Urgent issue uthana. Teen alag-alag cheezein hain!

💡 Quick Recall

No-Confidence aur Adjournment Motion dono mein minimum 50 members chahiye. Censure Motion mein koi minimum number nahi.

💰 Annual Financial Statement (Art. 112)
  • By President → Estimate of receipts & expenditure
  • Presented by → Finance Minister
  • Word "Budget" NOT mentioned in Constitution
  • First Budget → R.K. Shanmukham Chetty (26 Nov 1947)
  • Rajya Sabha → Can only discuss, no voting rights on budget
  • Only Lok Sabha has voting rights on budget
  • Consolidated Fund → Not votable (Salary/Allowances) Other items → Vote in LS only
⚠ Exam Trap

"Budget" word Constitution mein hai hi nahi — it's called Annual Financial Statement. RS budget pe vote NAHI kar sakti — sirf discuss kar sakti hai.

🏦 Three Types of Funds
Art. 266(1)
💛 Consolidated Fund of India
All receipts credited + all payments debited. No withdrawal without Parliament's approval. Salaries & allowances of constitutional posts.
Art. 267
🔴 Contingency Fund of India
Reserve for emergency expenditures. Held by President. Parliament approval taken later. Used for unforeseen urgent expenses.
Art. 266(2)
🟢 Public Accounts of India
Savings accounts of ministries. Includes Disaster Management Fund, Provident Fund, Defence Fund. Not subject to Parliamentary vote.
⚠ Exam Trap

Consolidated Fund se withdrawal ke liye Parliament ki permission zaroori hai. Contingency Fund se President bina Parliament ke withdraw kar sakta hai — baad mein Parliament se sanction lena hota hai.
