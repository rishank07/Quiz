📋 Key Articles
📖 General Facts
  • Head of the State (Chief of State)
  • Supreme Commander of Armed Forces
  • Holds all Executive Powers — but Nominal Executive Real Authority → Prime Minister + Council of Ministers
  • First Citizen of India
  • Chancellor of all Central Universities
⚠ Exam Trap

President "Nominal Executive" hai — real power Prime Minister ke paas hai. Isliye President ke decisions Cabinet ki advice pe hoti hain (Art. 74). "De Jure Executive" = President; "De Facto Executive" = PM.

  • Must be a citizen of India

💡 Quick Recall

India mein naturalized citizen bhi President ban sakta hai — USA mein nahi! USA ka President sirf natural-born citizen ho sakta hai.

📋 Other Important Details
  • Oath Administered by → Chief Justice of India (or senior-most judge)
  • Resignation To → Vice-President
  • Term → 5 years (resign / die / impeached)
  • Salary → ₹5 Lakh/month from Consolidated Fund of India
  • Re-election → Any number of times (no limit)
  • Conditions: Must not hold any office of profit under Union or State Govt.
  • Dispute in Election → Supreme Court (Article 71)
⚠ Exam Trap

President ka resignation Vice-President ko jaata hai, Speaker ko nahi. Oath CJI dilwate hain — Vice-President nahi. Ye dono popular confusions hain!

🗳️ Election Procedure
  • Indirect Election
  • Proportional Representation system
  • Single Transferable Vote
  • Secret Ballot
  • Security Deposit → ₹15,000/-
  • 50 Proposers + 50 Seconders required
  • Dispute Resolution → Supreme Court (Art. 71)
⚠ Exam Trap

President ka election Direct nahi, Indirect hai. Aam janta vote nahi karti — Electoral College vote karta hai. Yahi reason hai Presidential election aur Lok Sabha election alag hain.

⚡ Powers of the President
🟠 1. Executive Powers
  • Appoints Prime Minister & Council of Ministers
  • Appoints Governors of States
  • Appoints Judges of SC & HCs
  • Appoints Chairpersons of Commissions
  • Appoints Diplomats (Ambassadors, High Commissioners)
🔵 2. Legislative Powers
  • Summon & Prorogue Parliament sessions
  • Dissolution of Lok Sabha
  • Assent to bills — can withhold, return, or give assent
  • Issue Ordinances when Parliament not in session
🟢 3. Judicial Powers (Art. 72)
  • Pardon — completely absolve punishment
  • Reprieve — temporary stay of punishment
  • Respite — reduced sentence due to special reason
  • Remission — reduce quantum of punishment
🔴 4. Emergency Powers
  • National Emergency Art. 352
  • President's Rule in States Art. 356
  • Financial Emergency Art. 360
🎁 Pardoning Powers — Art. 72 (Detail)
Power Meaning
Pardon Completely absolve — saza khatam, conviction bhi khatam
Commutation Ek saza ko doosri (kam) saza mein badalna
Reprieve Temporary postponement — especially death sentence
Respite Special reason pe sentence reduce karna (e.g. pregnancy)
Remission Punishment ki quantity reduce karna (nature same rahta)
⚠ Exam Trap

Art. 72 = President ke pardoning powers. Art. 161 = Governor ke pardoning powers. Governor death sentence mein pardon nahi de sakta — sirf President de sakta hai!

💡 Quick Recall

Giani Zail Singh (1982–87) — Pocket Veto exercise ki, Postal Bill pe assent nahi diya. Pocket veto = na assent dena, na return karna.

⚖️ Impeachment Procedure (Art. 61)
  • Quasi-judicial process
  • Any House (Lok Sabha or Rajya Sabha) can initiate
  • 14-days prior notice required before resolution
  • Passed by Special Majority — not less than 2/3rd of Total Membership in both Houses
  • If passed in both Houses → President stands removed
⚠ Exam Trap

Impeachment mein Special Majority chahiye — 2/3rd of Total Membership (present & voting ki majority nahi). Aur dono Houses mein pass hona zaroori hai.

💡 Quick Recall

Abhi tak kisi bhi President ka impeachment nahi hua India mein. Ye sirf theoretical provision hai. No President has ever been impeached in India.
