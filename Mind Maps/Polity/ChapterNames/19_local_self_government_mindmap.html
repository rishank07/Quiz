⚠ Exam Trap

Lord Ripon's Resolution of 1882 — called "Magna Carta of Local Self-Government." Panchayati Raj actually started — 2nd October 1959 in Nagaur, Rajasthan by Jawaharlal Nehru. Dono dates alag hain!

📅 Key Historical Timeline
  • 1882 → Lord Ripon's Resolution — Magna Carta
  • 1952 → Community Development Scheme started
  • 1953 → National Extension Service Scheme
  • 1957 → Balwant Rai Mehta Committee
  • 1959 (Oct 2) → Panchayati Raj launched — Nagaur, Rajasthan Launched by Jawaharlal Nehru · Simultaneously in AP too
  • 1992 → 73rd & 74th Constitutional Amendments passed
  • 1993 (Apr 24) → 73rd Amendment effective — Panchayati Raj Diwas
  • 1993 (Jun 1) → 74th Amendment effective
  • 2010 → Panchayati Raj Diwas celebrations began
💡 Quick Recall — For BPSC

Bihar was the first state to implement 50% women reservation in Panchayats (2006). This was later adopted by 21 other states. Bihar pioneered women's political empowerment at grassroots!

📋 Committees Related to Panchayati Raj
1957 · ⭐ MOST IMPORTANT
Balwant Rai Mehta Committee
  • Architect of Panchayati Raj System
  • Proposed democratic decentralization
  • Recommended 3-Tier System: Village Panchayat (Village) · Kshetra Panchayat (Block) · District Panchayat (District)
1977–78
Ashok Mehta Committee
  • Recommended 2-Tier Structure
  • Mandala Panchayat — for 15,000–20,000 population
  • District Councils at district level
1986
L.M. Singhvi Committee
  • Recommended Constitutional Status to Panchayati Raj
  • Recommended setting up Nyaya Panchayats
1988–89
P.K. Thungan Committee
  • Timely elections to Panchayats
  • Fixed tenure of 5 years
1988
V.N. Gadgil Committee
  • Recommended direct election to all three tiers
⚠ Exam Trap — Balwant Rai vs Ashok Mehta

Balwant Rai Mehta (1957) = 3-tier system (implemented). Ashok Mehta (1977) = 2-tier system (NOT implemented). L.M. Singhvi (1986) ne Constitutional Status recommend kiya — jo 73rd Amendment se mila. Sequence yaad karo!

🌿 73rd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1992
73rd
Panchayati Raj — Constitutional Status
PM: P.V. Narasimha Rao · Effective: 24th April, 1993
Part: Part IX · Articles: 243–243O
Schedule: 11th Schedule added with 29 subjects
  • Gram Sabha → Art. 243(A) — all registered voters in village
  • 3-tier Panchayat → Art. 243(B)
  • Term → 5 years · Min. age → 21 years
  • Reservation → SC/ST proportional + 1/3rd for women
  • State Finance Commission → every 5 years
  • State Election Commission → elections conducted by SEC
⚠ Exam Trap

73rd Amendment ki effective date = 24 April 1993 (Panchayati Raj Diwas). 74th Amendment = 1 June 1993. Dono 1992 mein pass, 1993 mein effective. Min. age for Panchayat = 21 years (Parliament ke liye 25/30 — alag hai!).

🏙️ 74th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1992
74th
Municipalities — Constitutional Status
Effective: 1st June, 1993
Part: Part IXA · Articles: 243P–243ZG
Schedule: 12th Schedule added with 18 subjects
  • 1st Municipal Corporation → Established in Madras, 1687 Later via Charter Act of 1793 — Bombay, Calcutta, Madras
  • Ward Committees → Areas with population over 3 lakh Art 243(S)
  • District Planning Committees → Art 243ZD
  • Metropolitan Planning Committees → Art 243ZE — 10 lakh+ population
⚠ Exam Trap

1st Municipal Corporation = Madras (1687) — oldest. 12th Schedule = 18 subjects for Municipalities. Metropolitan Planning Committee = areas with 10 lakh (1 million)+ population.

📜 PESA Act, 1996
  • Full Name: Panchayats (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Act, 1996
  • Area under 5th Schedule (Tribal areas)
  • Recommended by Bhuria Commission
  • Presently implemented in 10 States
💡 Quick Recall

PESA (1996) extended Panchayat system to Scheduled (tribal) areas under 5th Schedule. These areas were excluded from 73rd Amendment's automatic coverage. 6th Schedule areas (NE states) have different tribal governance arrangements.

⚖️ Nyaya Panchayats
  • For villagers — village-level justice
  • Handles both Civil + Criminal cases
  • Punishment & fine → ₹50 to ₹1,000
  • Recommended by L.M. Singhvi Committee (1986)
⚠ Exam Trap

Nyaya Panchayat = Singhvi Committee (1986) ne recommend kiya. Gram Nyayalaya (Village Court) = Separate law — Gram Nyayalayas Act, 2008. Dono alag hain — Nyaya Panchayat (informal) vs Gram Nyayalaya (formal court under act).

🤝 Cooperative Societies
  • 97th Constitutional Amendment Act, 2011
  • Added Part IX(B) · Articles 243(ZH)–243(ZT)
  • Gave Constitutional Status to Cooperative Societies
  • Connected to Fundamental Right → Art. 19(c) — Right to form associations
  • Also added Art. 43B to DPSP (Gandhian principle)
💡 Quick Recall

97th Amendment (2011) — trifecta: (1) FR mein right to form cooperatives added, (2) DPSP mein Art. 43B added, (3) Part IX(B) add karke Constitutional status diya. Teen changes ek amendment mein!
