⚠ Exam Trap

Fundamental Duties ka source Soviet Union (Russia) hai — US ya Ireland nahi. Aur ye 42nd Amendment, 1976 se add hua, constitution ke original text mein nahi tha.

🔑 Key Facts at a Glance
  • Part IV-A — Constitution mein ek naya part add kiya gaya (IV-A)
  • Swaran Singh Committee — Indira Gandhi government ke time, 1976
  • Originally 8 duties recommend ki gayi thi committee ne; 10 add ki gayi
  • 42nd Amendment — "Mini Constitution" — sabse zyada changes is ek amendment mein
  • 86th Amendment, 2002 — 11th duty add ki + Art. 21A + Art. 45 modify hua
  • Applicable to Indian Citizens only — foreigners pe nahi
  • Courts impose nahi kar sakti lekin guidance ke liye use hoti hain
💡 Quick Recall

Verma Committee (1999) ne Fundamental Duties ke implementation pe report di. Iske baad legal provisions strengthened hue FDs ko support karne ke liye.

8
To develop scientific temper, humanism, & the spirit of inquiry and reform.
Scientific Temper · Humanism

To strive towards excellence in all spheres of individual & collective activity.

To provide opportunities for education to children between the age of 6 and 14 years.
Education · 6–14 Years
🆕 86th Amend. 2002
⚠ Exam Trap

11th Duty — 86th Amendment 2002 se add hua. Ye parents/guardians pe duty hai ki woh apne 6–14 saal ke bachon ko education dilayein. Isko Art. 21A (Right to Education FR) ke sath link karo — dono same amendment se aaye!

💡 Quick Recall

Duty no. 7 (Environment) ko Art. 48A (DPSP) se link karo — dono environment protection ke baare mein hain. Duty ka source citizen hai, DPSP ka source State hai.

Teen sources yaad karo — FR = USA, FD = Soviet Union (Russia), DPSP = Ireland. Ye combination BPSC mein directly pucha jaata hai MCQ form mein!

💡 Quick Recall

FD non-justiciable hain lekin courts inhe interpret karne mein use karti hain. Example: Flag Code of India 2002 ne Duty 1 ko implement kiya, Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act ne Duty 7 ko implement kiya.

🧠 High-Frequency Exam Points
  • 42nd Amendment, 1976 → Inserted FDs (Art. 51A) with initially 10 duties
  • 86th Amendment, 2002 → Added 11th duty (education 6–14 yrs)
  • Swaran Singh Committee → Recommended FDs (suggested 8, added 10)
  • Source → Soviet Union / Russia
  • Part IV-A — not Part IV (that's DPSP) and not Part III (that's FR)
  • FDs are for Citizens only — foreigners exempt
  • FDs are Non-Justiciable — court enforce nahi kar sakti
  • Duty 7 (environment) ↔ Art. 48A DPSP (state duty for environment) — parallel
  • Duty 11 ↔ Art. 21A FR ↔ RTE Act 2009 — all three connected
⚠ Exam Trap

Swaran Singh Committee ne 8 duties suggest ki thi, lekin government ne 10 add ki. Aur baad mein 86th Amendment se 11th bhi add hui. Ye number confusion bahut common question hai!
