Type 1 — Finding 25% and 50% of Numbers
25% = 1/4, 50% = 1/2
25% of 48 = 1/4 × 48 = 12 50% of 642 = 1/2 × 642 = 321
Type 2 — Finding 10% and 20% of Numbers
10% = 1/10, 20% = 1/5
10% of 450 = 1/10 × 450 = 45 20% of 655 = 1/5 × 655 = 131
⚡ QUICK RECALL
10% = shift decimal 1 left. 20% = 10% doubled. 25% = ÷4. 50% = ÷2. Yeh sabse common exam percentages hain — instant honi chahiye.
Type 3 — Finding 40% and 60% of Numbers
40% = 2/5, 60% = 3/5
40% of 65 = 2/5 × 65 = 26 60% of 125 = 3/5 × 125 = 75
Type 4 — Finding 75% and 80% of Numbers
75% = 3/4, 80% = 4/5
75% of 48 = 3/4 × 48 = 36 80% of 225 = 4/5 × 225 = 180
⚡ QUICK RECALL
Sab "/5" family (20%,40%,60%,80%) → divide by 5, multiply by numerator. "/4" family (25%,75%) → divide by 4.
Type 5 — Finding 16.66% and 33.33% of Numbers
16.66% = 1/6, 33.33% = 1/3
16.66% of 246 = 1/6 × 246 = 41 33.33% of 810 = 1/3 × 810 = 270
⚠ EXAM TRAP
16.66% aur 33.33% dono "Point%" family hain — inka exact fraction repeating decimal se aata hai (1/6=0.1666..., 1/3=0.3333...). Exam mein rounded % dikhta hai, lekin fraction EXACT use karo.
Finding 6.66% and 6.25% of Numbers
6.66% = 1/15, 6.25% = 1/16
6.66% of 225 = 1/15 × 225 = 15 6.25% of 128 = 1/16 × 128 = 8
Finding 12.5% and 14.28% of Numbers
12.5% = 1/8, 14.28% = 1/7
12.5% of 488 = 1/8 × 488 = 61 14.28% of 154 = 1/7 × 154 = 22
⚡ QUICK RECALL
1/16=6.25% | 1/15=6.66% | 1/8=12.5% | 1/7=14.28% — inko ek dusre se confuse mat karo, denominators bahut close hain.
Finding 37.5% and 7.14% of Numbers
37.5% = 3/8, 7.14% = 1/14
37.5% of 64 = 3/8 × 64 = 24 7.14% of 350 = 1/14 × 350 = 25
Finding 62.5% and 9.09% of Numbers
62.5% = 5/8, 9.09% = 1/11
62.5% of 32 = 5/8 × 32 = 20 9.09% of 473 = 1/11 × 473 = 43
⚡ QUICK RECALL
7.14% = half of 14.28% (both from 1/7 family: 1/14 and 1/7). 9.09% comes from 1/11 — connects to Ch05 Division Type-7 (÷11 family).
Type 10 — Finding 11.11% of Numbers
11.11% = 1/9
11.11% of 819 = 1/9 × 819 = 91
Type 12 — Deriving New Percentages from Known Fractions
Multiply a known base fraction to get related percentages
Example: 22.22% (derived from 11.11%)
11.11% = 1/9 So 22.22% = 2 × (1/9) = 2/9
Example: 7.14% (derived from 14.28%)
14.28% = 1/7 So 7.14% (half of 14.28%) = 1/14
⚡ QUICK RECALL
Agar % ka double/half/multiple ek known fraction se related dikhe, to naya fraction bhi usi anupaat mein derive kar lo — nayi table yaad karne ki zaroorat nahi.
Type 11 — Dal Badlu Approach (Reversal Trick)
Rule: a% of b = b% of a — swap which number carries the % symbol
Example: 24% of 25
Rewrite as: 25% of 24 Step 1: 25% = 1/4 Step 2: 1/4 × 24 = 6
⚠ EXAM TRAP
"Dal Badlu" = swap kar do — jab % wala number "ugly" ho (jaise 24%) lekin doosra number "nice" fraction bana sake (jaise 25=1/4), to hamesha swap karo. Answer same rehta hai, calculation asaan ho jaata hai.
⚡ QUICK RECALL
112% of 12.5 = 12.5% of 112 = (1/8)×112 = 14 — is tarah bade-lagne-wale % questions turant simple ho jaate hain.
