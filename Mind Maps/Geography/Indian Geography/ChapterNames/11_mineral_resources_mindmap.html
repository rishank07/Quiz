🔩 Manganese (Mn)
🔩 Manganese — Key Facts
  • Reserves: Odisha → Karnataka (Shivamogga) → MP
  • Odisha areas: Keonjhar, Bonal, Kalahandi, Sundergarh
  • Production: MP → Maharashtra → Odisha
  • MP areas: Jamanpani, Tirodi, Poniya (Balaghat)
  • World #1 producer: South Africa
State-wise Regions:
State Regions
Odisha Bolangir, Ponchpatmali Hills (Koraput), Kalahandi
Madhya Pradesh Bindoni (Mandla), Amarkantak (Shahdol), Kareli
Jharkhand Lohardaga, Palamu, Ranchi
🚨 EXAM TRAP
  • Magnetite = most pure iron (72%) but reserves in Karnataka
  • Hematite = most common iron (60-70%) — reserves in Odisha
  • Manganese reserves #1 = Odisha | production #1 = MP — alag!
  • Noamundi + Gua + Jamda = Jharkhand (Singhbhum) iron ore
  • Manganese in Jharkhand = Lohardaga, Palamu, Ranchi

📍 Bihar & Jharkhand — Mineral Belt
⚠️ Note: Jharkhand was Bihar before 2000
  • Jharkhand carved out from Bihar on 15 November 2000
  • Most minerals of "Bihar" in older texts = now in Jharkhand
  • BPSC exams still ask about Jharkhand minerals in Bihar context
🖤 Coal — Jharkhand's Biggest Mineral
  • Coal Reserves India #1 = Jharkhand
  • Jharia = largest bituminous coal region in India
  • Major coal areas: Jharia, Bokaro, Karnpura, Chandrapura, Giridih, Rajmahal
  • Damodar Valley = Gondwana Coal = India's richest coal belt
  • Thermal plants: Bokaro, Chandrapura, Patratu (all in Jharkhand)
🔩 Iron Ore — Singhbhum
  • Iron ore in Jharkhand: Singhbhum district
  • Areas: Gua, Jamda, Noamundi
  • Chotanagpur Plateau = Archaean rocks = mineral rich base
✨ Mica — Kodarma Belt
  • Kodarma–Gaya–Hazaribagh = major mica belt
  • Kodarma = in Jharkhand (Gaya = Bihar border)
  • Mica use: electrical insulator in electronics
  • Mica gives Jharkhand significant industrial importance
🔩 Manganese & Copper
  • Manganese in Jharkhand: Lohardaga, Palamu, Ranchi
  • Copper in Jharkhand: Mausabani, Rakha, Pathargora
  • Jharkhand = reserves #3 in copper
☢️ Uranium — Jaduguda
  • Jaduguda — major uranium mine of Jharkhand
  • India's first uranium mine
  • Uranium producing states: Jharkhand, AP, Rajasthan
⚡ BPSC MASTER RECALL — Jharkhand/Bihar Minerals
  • Coal reserves #1 = Jharkhand | Jharia = largest bituminous belt
  • Iron ore = Singhbhum (Gua, Noamundi)
  • Mica = Kodarma–Gaya–Hazaribagh belt
  • Manganese = Lohardaga, Palamu, Ranchi
  • Copper = Mausabani, Rakha
  • Uranium = Jaduguda (India's first uranium mine)
  • Damodar Valley = Gondwana coal = richest region
  • DVC thermal plants: Bokaro, Chandrapura, Patratu
🚨 BPSC EXAM TRAP
  • Jharkhand = coal reserves #1 but production #1 = Odisha
  • Jharia = bituminous coal (NOT anthracite — anthracite = J&K)
  • Jaduguda = Uranium (NOT coal or iron ore)
  • Kodarma is in Jharkhand (Gaya is in Bihar — borderland mica belt)
  • Son River Valley also has coal deposits — important for Bihar
