🏆 9 Major Multipurpose Projects
1️⃣ Bhakra Nangal Project — Satluj River
  • River: Satluj River
  • Joint venture: Punjab + Haryana + Rajasthan
  • Bhakra → Himachal Pradesh | Nangal → Punjab
  • Govind Sagar Lake (artificial) → constructed 1962
  • World's largest gravity dam — Height: 226 m, Length: 518 m
2️⃣ Beas Project — Beas + Satluj
  • At confluence of Beas + Satluj
  • Beneficiaries: Rajasthan, Punjab, Haryana, HP
  • Pong Dam → Dhauladhar Hills, Beas River
⚡ Indira Gandhi Canal
Length: 649 km | Origin: Harike Barrage (Sutlej + Beas confluence) | India's longest canal | Brings water to arid Rajasthan, Haryana, Punjab
3️⃣ Damodar Valley Project — India's FIRST (1948)
  • River: Damodar River
  • States: Jharkhand + West Bengal
  • India's first multipurpose river valley project — 1948
  • Modeled on USA's Tennessee Valley Project
  • Thermal Plants: Bokaro, Durgapur, Chandrapura, Patratu
  • 8 dams built on Damodar and tributaries

4️⃣ Rihand Dam — UP
  • River: Rihand River
  • Largest project of UP
  • Reservoir: Govind Ballabh Pant Sagar (India's largest artificial lake)
  • Power: 300 MW
5️⃣ Pancheshwar Project — Indo-Nepal
  • River: Kali River
  • Indo-Nepal joint project
  • Benefits: Bihar, UP, Uttarakhand
  • Objectives: Electricity, irrigation, flood control
6️⃣ Koyna Project — Maharashtra
  • River: Koyna River
  • District: Satara, Maharashtra
  • Hydroelectric project — 1962-63
  • Underground power house
7️⃣ Baglihar Project — J&K
  • River: Chenab River
  • State: Jammu & Kashmir
  • Power: 900 MW
8️⃣ Mayurakshi Project — JH + WB
  • River: Mayurakshi River
  • Also known as Canada Dam
  • Joint: Jharkhand + West Bengal
  • Hydroelectric + Irrigation
9️⃣ Sardar Sarovar — Narmada
  • River: Narmada River
  • States: Gujarat, Maharashtra, MP, Rajasthan
  • Reservoir: Indira Sagar Dam — India's largest dam by volume

📍 Bihar Special — Multipurpose Projects
😢 Kosi Project — Flood Control
  • River: Kosi River
  • State: Bihar
  • Objective: Flood control (Kosi = Sorrow of Bihar)
  • Embankments + Barrages built to control devastating floods
🤝 Gandak River Project — Indo-Nepal Joint
  • River: Gandak River
  • Joint Indo-Nepal project
  • Benefits: Bihar + UP
  • Gandak meets Ganga at Hajipur, Bihar
  • Provides irrigation water to large parts of North Bihar
🤝 Pancheshwar Project — Benefits Bihar
  • River: Kali River
  • Indo-Nepal joint project
  • Benefits: Bihar, UP, Uttarakhand
  • Objectives: electricity, irrigation, flood control in Bihar
🏗️ Son River Projects — Bihar Connection
  • Bansagar Dam → Son River (MP) → supplies water to Bihar also
  • Son meets Ganga at Patna (right bank)
  • Son Canal System — major irrigation in Bihar
  • Son tributaries: Koel, Rihand (Jharkhand se aate hain)
🌊 Farakka Barrage — Impact on Bihar
  • Location: West Bengal — Ganga + Bhagirathi River
  • Purpose: Maintain navigability of Kolkata port, divert water to Bhagirathi
  • Bihar impact: Backwater effect → increased flooding in South Bihar (Patna, Bhojpur area)
  • Long-standing dispute between Bihar and WB over Farakka flooding
⚡ DVC — Jharkhand-Bihar Region
  • Damodar Valley project → Jharkhand + WB
  • Thermal plants: Bokaro (JH), Patratu (JH) — near Bihar border
  • DVC power supplied to parts of Bihar also
  • Chotanagpur Plateau area (JH) = coal belt → thermal power
⚡ BPSC MASTER RECALL — Projects related to Bihar
  • Kosi Project → flood control, Bihar
  • Gandak Project → Indo-Nepal joint, Bihar+UP
  • Pancheshwar → Kali River, Indo-Nepal, benefits Bihar
  • Son River system → irrigation in Bihar (origin: Amarkantak, MP)
  • Farakka Barrage → WB, but affects Bihar's flooding
  • Bansagar Dam → Son River (MP) — upstream of Bihar
🚨 BPSC EXAM TRAP — Bihar Projects
  • Kosi Project ≠ Kosi dam — it's a barrage + embankment system
  • Gandak Project = Indo-Nepal (joint) — NOT purely Indian
  • Pancheshwar = Kali River — NOT Kosi (different rivers!)
  • Farakka is in WB but Bihar mein baadh badhata hai — BPSC ka favourite point
  • "Temple of Modern India" = Nehru ka quote — sabhi dams ke liye, kisi ek ke liye nahi
