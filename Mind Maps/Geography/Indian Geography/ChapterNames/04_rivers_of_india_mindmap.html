💧 Drainage Patterns
🌐 5 Drainage Patterns
  • Radial — Flow in all directions from a mountain
  • Dendritic — Like tree branches
  • Parallel — Rivers flow parallel to each other
  • Trellis — Right angle confluence of rivers
  • Centripetal — Rivers falling into lake/depression
📊 2 Drainage Systems
  • Himalayan Drainage System
    • Perennial rivers (snow-fed)
    • Antecedent rivers mostly
    • Deep V-shaped valleys, gorges, waterfalls
  • Peninsular Drainage System
    • Seasonal rivers (rain-fed)
    • Fixed course, no meandering
    • Flow through rift valleys
    • Formed in Early Tertiary Period

🌊 Yamuna & Its Tributaries
  • Origin: Yamunotri Glacier (Bandarpunch Range)
  • Length: 1376 km
  • Confluence: Ganga at Prayagraj
  • Tributaries: Chambal, Sindh, Ken, Betwa, Tons
  • Water mostly used for irrigation
  • Delhi Ridge = water divide between Indus and Ganga rivers
Chambal River (Yamuna's main trib):
  • Origin: Mhow, Malwa Plateau (MP)
  • Known for badland topography
  • Tribs: Parvati, Kali, Sindh, Shipra
  • Flows north of Tropic of Cancer
  • Banas river → from Aravalli Hills (Rajasthan)
😢 Kosi River — Sorrow of Bihar
  • Origin: Tibet (antecedent river)
  • Length: 730 km
  • Main stream in Tibet: Arun
  • Arun joins to form Saptakosi
  • Tributaries: Sun Kosi, Dudh Kosi, Tamur
⚠️ "Sorrow of Bihar" — Kosi river changes its course frequently → floods Bihar every year → massive devastation → hence "Sorrow of Bihar"
🌊 Sarda / Ghagara / Son Key Facts
  • Sarda (Saryu): Origin Milam Glacier (Nepal) | 350 km | Known as Gauriganga in Nepal | India-Nepal border river
  • Ghagara: Origin Mapchachungo Glacier | 1080 km | Meets Ganga at Chapra, Bihar | Trib: Sarda, Sarju
  • Son: Origin Amarkantak Hills | 780 km | Meets Ganga at Patna | Tribs: Koel, Rihand
🚨 EXAM TRAP
  • Ganga formed at Devprayag (Alaknanda + Bhagirathi)
  • Largest trib = Yamuna | First trib = Ramganga | Last trib = Mahananda
  • Gandak meets Ganga at Hajipur (Bihar) — Patna ke saamne!
  • Son meets Ganga at Patna | Punpun at Fatuha (Patna)
  • Yamuna origin = Bandarpunch Range (Yamunotri Glacier) — NOT Gangotri
  • Ganga basin = India's largest drainage system (8.6 lakh km²)

  • U-turn near Namcha Barwa — yahi se India mein aati hai
  • Kolkata = Hoogly pe (Ganga pe nahi!)

  • Tributaries: Sun Kosi, Dudh Kosi, Tamur

  • Origin: Amarkantak Hills (MP)
  • Length: 780 km
  • Meets Ganga at: Patna (Dinapur)
  • Tributaries: Koel, Rihand
  • Flows through: MP → Jharkhand → Bihar
🌿 Gandak River — Nepal to Hajipur
  • Formed by: Kaligandak + Trishulganga (Nepal)
  • Origin: Nepal (between Dhaulagiri and Everest)
  • Length: 630 km
  • Meets Ganga: Hajipur, Bihar
🌊 Punpun River — Adi Ganga
  • Origin: Palamu district, Jharkhand
  • Length: 200 km
  • Meets Ganga at: Fatuha (near Patna)
  • Also known as: "Adi Ganga"
🌊 Ghagara / Sarda — Left Bank Tributaries
  • Ghagara Origin: Mapchachungo Glacier | Length: 1080 km
  • Meets Ganga: Chapra, Bihar
  • Tributary: Sarda River (Kali Ganga) — flows along India-Nepal border
  • Sarda also called Saryu | Origin: Milam Glacier | known as Gauriganga in Nepal
🏔️ Subarnarekha & Damodar — Near Bihar
  • Subarnarekha: Origin Chotanagpur Plateau → JH (Singhbhum) → Odisha → WB
  • Damodar: Origin Chotanagpur Plateau → "Sorrow of Bengal" → meets Hoogly
  • Brahmani: Formed by Koel + Shankha rivers (Odisha)
⚡ BIHAR MASTER RECALL
  • Ganga flows 445 km in Bihar
  • Kosi = Sorrow of Bihar (changes course, Tibet origin, antecedent)
  • Gandak → Hajipur | Ghagara → Chapra | Son → Patna | Punpun → Fatuha
  • Punpun = Adi Ganga | Son tribs = Koel + Rihand
  • Sarda = India-Nepal border river | Also called Saryu / Gauriganga
  • Damodar (Jharkhand) → "Sorrow of Bengal" | Chotanagpur Plateau origin
🚨 BPSC EXAM TRAP — Bihar Rivers
  • Gandak meets Ganga at Hajipur — NOT Patna (Patna is opposite bank = Vaishali district)
  • Son meets Ganga at Patna (right bank)
  • Kosi origin = Tibet | Main stream in Tibet = Arun
  • Punpun = Adi Ganga = meets at Fatuha — ye naam yaad rakho
  • Ghaghara = 1080 km — longest tributary of Ganga
