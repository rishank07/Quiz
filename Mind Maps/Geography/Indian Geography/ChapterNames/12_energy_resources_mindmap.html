📊 Two Types of Energy
🔒 Conventional / Non-Renewable
  • Not reusable | Limited availability
  • Examples: Coal, Petroleum, Natural Gas, Thermal, Nuclear, Shale Gas
  • Installed capacity (June 2025): 235.7 GW (49%)
♻️ Non-Conventional / Renewable
  • Reusable | Unlimited availability
  • Examples: Biogas, Solar, Geo-thermal, Tidal, Hydro
  • Installed capacity (June 2025): 242.78 GW (49%)

🛢️ Petroleum & Oil Refineries
🛢️ Petroleum — Black Gold
  • India's first oil reserve: Digboi, Assam
  • First crude oil pipeline: Naharkatiya–Nunmati–Barauni Pipeline
  • Major areas: Mumbai High, Ankleshwar (Gujarat), Digboi (Assam)
  • Top producers: Rajasthan, Gujarat, Assam
Oil Refineries:
Sector Refineries
Public Guwahati, Barauni★, Koyali, Haldia, Mathura, Digboi
Joint Bina (Bharat Oman Refinery), Bhatinda
Private Jamnagar, SEZ Jamnagar, Vadinar
💨 Natural Gas
  • Clean energy | Present in petroleum fields
  • Major reserves: Krishna-Godavari Basin (KG Basin), Gulf of Khambhat
  • Top producers: Assam, Rajasthan, Tripura
  • HVJ Pipeline: Hazira–Vijaipur–Jagdishpur — India's first cross-country natural gas pipeline
Shale Gas:
  • Found between sedimentary rocks
  • Abundant in: Bengal Basin, Damodar Basin, Vindhya Basin
🚨 EXAM TRAP
  • Barauni oil refinery = Bihar (public sector) — BPSC most asked!
  • First pipeline = Naharkatiya–Nunmati–Barauni (ends in Bihar!)
  • Digboi = India's first oil reserve (Assam)
  • Shale gas = sedimentary rocks ke beech (NOT igneous)
  • Natural gas top = Assam (#1) NOT Rajasthan

📍 Bihar & Jharkhand — Energy Resources
🛢️ Barauni Oil Refinery — Bihar's Most Important
  • Location: Barauni, Bihar
  • Sector: Public Sector
  • India's first crude oil pipeline ended here: Naharkatiya–Nunmati–Barauni
  • Connected to Assam's Naharkatiya oil fields
  • BPSC mein sabse zyada poochha jaane wala energy fact about Bihar!
🖤 Coal — Jharkhand's Crown
  • Jharkhand = India's largest coal reserves
  • Jharia = largest bituminous coal region in India
  • Major coal areas: Jharia, Bokaro, Karnpura, Chandrapura, Giridih, Rajmahal
  • Gondwana coal = 98% of India's coal production
  • Damodar Valley = Gondwana coal belt = JH + WB
🏭 Thermal & Power Plants near Bihar-JH
  • DVC Thermal Plants: Bokaro, Chandrapura, Patratu (Jharkhand)
  • Coal → Thermal Power → supplied to Bihar + WB region
  • Raniganj coal belt (WB) also near Bihar-JH corridor
💧 Hydro — Tilaiya (Jharkhand)
  • Tilaiya Ultra Mega Power Project — Jharkhand
  • Barakar River — part of Damodar Valley
  • Also: DVC dams generate hydroelectricity
💨 Shale Gas — Damodar Basin
  • Shale gas deposits in Damodar Basin (JH + WB)
  • Also in: Bengal Basin, Vindhya Basin
  • Future energy potential for Jharkhand
⚡ BPSC MASTER RECALL — Bihar/JH Energy
  • Bihar = Barauni Oil Refinery (public sector, first pipeline endpoint)
  • Jharkhand = largest coal reserves in India
  • Jharia = largest bituminous coal region (Jharkhand)
  • Tilaiya = hydro project, Jharkhand
  • DVC thermal plants: Bokaro, Patratu, Chandrapura (JH)
  • Shale gas = Damodar Basin (JH + WB)
🚨 BPSC EXAM TRAP
  • Barauni = Bihar (public sector refinery) — NOT Jharkhand
  • First pipeline = Naharkatiya–Nunmati–Barauni (originates Assam, ends Bihar)
  • Coal reserves #1 = Jharkhand | Production #1 = Chhattisgarh
  • Tilaiya in Jharkhand — hydro (Barakar River) — exam mein poochha jaata hai
