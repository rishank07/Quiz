⛏️ Group 1 — Archaean
🌏 Archaean System — Most Ancient Rocks
  • Contribute ~86.7% in formation of the Earth
  • Formed during cooling of the Earth
  • No Fossils — Pre-biological era
  • Rock types: Gneiss and Schist
Bundelkhand Gneiss (most ancient)
Bengal Gneiss

🏔️ Dharwar System of Rocks
  • Formed by erosion & deposition of Archaean rocks
  • Most developed in Dharwar region, Karnataka
  • No Fossils
  • Aravalli mountain range — formed from these rocks
⚡ QUICK RECALL
Dharwar rocks → Aravalli mountains bane | Most developed in Karnataka (Dharwar district)
🚨 EXAM TRAP
  • Bundelkhand Gneiss = most ancient rocks of India
  • Archaean = 86.7% Earth formation — sabse zyada contribution
  • Aravalli = Dharwar rocks se bana, Archaean se nahi
📦 Group 2 — Purana
🔵 Cuddapah System
  • Formed by erosion of Archaean + Dharwar rocks
  • Layered rocks (sedimentary)
  • No fossils — but origin of life on Earth
  • Named after Cuddapah district, Andhra Pradesh
  • Eastern Ghats — formed from these rocks
🟣 Vindhyan System
  • Plateau — divides Ganga Plain from Deccan
  • Layered, formed by water deposition
  • General absence of fossils
  • Minerals: limestone, sandstone, china clay, copper clay
📊 Cuddapah vs Vindhyan — Comparison
Feature Cuddapah System Vindhyan System
Formation Archaean + Dharwar erosion Water deposition
Fossils Absent (origin of life) Generally absent
Landform Eastern Ghats Vindhya Plateau
Named After Cuddapah district (AP) Vindhya mountains
Minerals — Limestone, Sandstone, Clays
🚨 EXAM TRAP
  • Eastern Ghats = Cuddapah rocks se bane
  • Vindhyan rocks = Ganga Plain aur Deccan ka dividing line
  • Cuddapah = AP ke district ka naam — ye rocks mostly AP mein milte hain
🦕 Group 3 — Dravidian
🦴 Paleozoic Era Rocks
  • Rocks of Paleozoic Era
  • First evidence of life on Earth
  • Has rich fossils — sabse zyada fossils
  • Absent from almost whole India
  • Found only in: Rewa district, Madhya Pradesh
🚨 EXAM TRAP — Most Asked!
Dravidian Group = almost absent from India — sirf Rewa (MP) mein milte hain. Lekin sabse important = First/Rich Fossils. Ye contradiction yaad rakho!
⚡ QUICK RECALL
Dravidian = Paleozoic Era | Rich Fossils = First life evidence | Location = Only Rewa, MP
🔥 Group 4 — Aryan (4 Sub-Systems)
🪨 1. Gondwana System — Coal Treasure
  • Construction of basin landforms
  • Fossils present
  • ~98% of India's coal reserves found here!

🌋 2. Deccan Trap System — Black Soil Origin
  • End of Cretaceous Era → Volcanic activity in western Peninsular India
  • Basalt lava flow → step-like landform (trap = step)
  • Mainly in Maharashtra and Malwa region
  • Also formed: Rajmahal Trap
  • Basalt erosion → Fertile Black Soil (Regur)
Deccan Trap States:
Maharashtra
MP
Gujarat
Karnataka
Andhra Pradesh
⚡ QUICK RECALL
Deccan Trap → Basalt Lava → Black Soil (Regur/Cotton Soil) → Best for cotton cultivation
🏔️ 3. Tertiary System
  • Formed: Eocene to Pliocene epoch
  • Formation of Peninsular Plateau
  • Formation of Himalayan mountains
🌊 4. Quaternary System
  • Rocks of Pleistocene + Holocene epoch
  • Deposits at mouths of Indo-Gangetic Plains
  • Also in Deltaic regions
  • Most recent rocks
🚨 EXAM TRAP
  • Coal = Gondwana rocks mein — 98% India ka coal yahaan
  • Black soil = Deccan Trap basalt se — NOT Gondwana
  • "Trap" word comes from Swedish "trappa" = stairs/steps — step-like lava landform
  • Damodar Valley = Gondwana coal belt = Bihar + Jharkhand ka area (Bihar relevant!)

  • No fossils → Arch, Dhar, Cudd, Vindh (4 oldest)
