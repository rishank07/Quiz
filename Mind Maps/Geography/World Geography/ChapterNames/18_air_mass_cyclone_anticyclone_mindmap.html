🌬️ Air Mass
  • A large body of air uniformly related to temperature and humidity
🌦️ Front
  • The zone between cold and warm air masses
📂 Types of Fronts

Warm Front

Warm air advances over cold air • Gradual lifting • Steady rainfall

Cold Front

Cold air advances under warm air • Steep lifting • Heavy rainfall

Stationary Front

Neither warm nor cold air advances • Boundary remains stationary

Occluded Front

Cold front catches warm front • Warm air is lifted completely off the ground

📌 Frontogenesis Process of formation of a front
📌 Frontolysis Dissipation of a front, as one air mass overrides the other
⚡ Quick Recall
Mid-latitude / Temperate cyclones form due to frontogenesis — front formation is central to their development, unlike tropical cyclones.
🌀 Cyclone → A Low Pressure Zone
Key FeatureDetails
CentreLow pressure
PeripheryHigh pressure • Wind flows from periphery to centre
DirectionNorthern Hemisphere → Anti-clockwise • Southern Hemisphere → Clockwise (Coriolis force)
Eye of the CycloneCentre – calm zone • No rainfall in the eye
✅ Essential Conditions for Tropical Cyclone
  • High sea surface temperature
  • Coriolis force must be present — absent at the equator → no cyclone at the equator
  • Less variation in vertical air movement
  • Low pressure area
📌 Absence of cyclones on the Equator Coriolis force value is Zero there
⚠ Exam Trap
Cyclones need Coriolis force to spin, but Coriolis force is zero at the equator — that's exactly why cyclones never form right on the equator, despite warm ocean waters there.
📂 Types of Cyclones

Tropical Cyclone

  • Between Tropic of Cancer and Tropic of Capricorn
  • Occurs only over sea surface
  • More destructive
  • Moves East to West

Temperate / Extra-Tropical Cyclone

  • Between 35°–65° Latitudes (both hemispheres)
  • Occurs on both sea and land surface
  • Less destructive, generally
  • Moves West to East

⚠ Exam Trap
Tropical Cyclones move East to West (driven by Trade Winds); Temperate Cyclones move West to East (driven by Westerlies) — an easily reversed fact.
☀️ Anti-Cyclone → A High Pressure Zone
  • Opposite of a cyclone — a "weather-less" phenomenon
  • Centre: High air pressure • Wind flows from centre to periphery
  • Periphery: Low air pressure
  • Direction – Northern Hemisphere: Clockwise
  • Direction – Southern Hemisphere: Anti-clockwise

⚡ Quick Recall
Cyclone and Anti-Cyclone have exactly opposite wind rotation in each hemisphere — if a cyclone spins anti-clockwise in the NH, the anti-cyclone spins clockwise there.
