📐 Latitude
  • Imaginary horizontal lines drawn on the globe (East to West)
  • Angular distance of a place North or South from the Equator
  • Distance between latitudes is equal / Parallel
  • Distance between two latitude lines – 111 km
  • Total latitudes – 181 • Latitude lines/circles at the interval of 1° – 179 (excluding the two poles)
📌 Use of Latitude Useful in locating a place and determining climatic zones

⚡ Quick Recall

🌡️ Temperature Zones

Torrid Zone

Region between Tropic of Cancer and Tropic of Capricorn

Temperate Zone

Region between Tropic of Cancer / Capricorn and the Arctic / Antarctic circles (in respective hemispheres)

Frigid Zone

Region between Arctic Circle and North Pole (Northern Hemisphere) / Region between Antarctic Circle and South Pole (Southern Hemisphere)

⚠ Exam Trap
Torrid Zone lies entirely between the two Tropics — don't confuse it with the Temperate Zone, which lies beyond the Tropics up to the Polar Circles.

📍 Longitude
  • Prime Meridian (0° Longitude) or Greenwich Line – reference for angular distance of any place
  • Imaginary lines joining North and South Pole
  • Lines are not parallel
  • Distance between longitudes increases from poles towards the equator
  • Minimum distance – at poles • Maximum distance – 111.32 km (at the Equator)
  • Total Longitudes – 360
📌 Measurement unit Degree, Minute and Second
📌 Distance between two longitude lines Called a "Gusset"
🇬🇧 Prime Meridian / Greenwich Mean Time
  • Passes through Greenwich Observatory (London)
  • Passes through 8 countries spanning 3 continents — Europe, Africa and Antarctica
United KingdomFranceSpainAlgeria MaliBurkina FasoTogoGhana
📌 0° Latitude × 0° Longitude intersect In the Atlantic Ocean (Gulf of Guinea)
⚡ Quick Recall
Mnemonic for Greenwich-line countries: "TVM SAB FRANCE GAYE" — Togo, (UK), Mali, Spain, Algeria, Burkina Faso, France, Ghana.
🕒 Time Determination / Standard Time
  • Earth rotates 360° in 24 hours
  • In 1 hour = 15° (360° ÷ 24 = 15°)
  • Time taken to rotate 1° = 4 minutes
  • Increase in time for going east of 0° Longitude
  • Decrease in time for going west of 0° Longitude
  • Time difference for 1° longitude: 4 minutes

📌 Basis of World's Standard Time Greenwich Mean Time (GMT)
⚠ Exam Trap
India is ahead of GMT by 5 hours 30 minutes (not behind) since 82½°E lies east of the Prime Meridian.
🌐 International Date Line (IDL)
  • Location: 180° Longitude → Zig-Zag path
  • One day difference between East and West of the line
  • Runs parallel to the Bering Strait
🔁 Crossing the IDL

Going West (East → West)

  • Day is LOST
  • Date is GAINED

Going East (West → East)

  • Day is GAINED
  • Date is LOST
📌 Important Facts
  • World is divided into 24 time zones
  • France has the most time zones – 12 (due to overseas territories)
  • When it is 12 noon on the Greenwich meridian, sunrise (local 6 AM) occurs at 90° West longitude
⚡ Quick Recall
Mnemonic: "FEST" — IDL zig-zags to avoid splitting countries, especially around Russia/Alaska and Pacific island nations.
🌎 Hemispheres

Horizontal Division — by Equator

  • Northern Hemisphere (0° to 90° North Pole)
  • Southern Hemisphere (0° to 90° South Pole)

Vertical Division — by Prime Meridian & IDL

  • Eastern Hemisphere
  • Western Hemisphere
⚡ Quick Recall
Equator → splits Earth North/South · Prime Meridian + IDL → splits Earth East/West
