📖 Geography — Meaning & Origin
  • Geography = Geo (Earth) + Graphia (Description) → "Description of the Earth"
  • First use of the term 'Geography' by Eratosthenes (Greek Scholar)
276–139
BCE — Eratosthenes' Lifetime
⚡ Quick Recall
Geography term coined by Eratosthenes — also remembered as "Father of Systematic Geography / Geophysics".
⚠ Exam Trap
Don't confuse Father of Geography (Hecataeus) with Father of Systematic Geography (Eratosthenes) — both are frequently swapped in MCQs.
🌐 Branches of Geography

1. Physical Geography

Founder: Polybius

Study of landforms, soil, climate, etc.

  • Climatology
  • Geomorphology
  • Pedology
  • Hydrology
  • Biogeography

2. Human Geography

Founder: Friedrich Ratzel (Book: Anthropogeography)

Study of culture, cities, population, etc.

  • Economic Geography
  • Political Geography
  • Population Geography
  • Behavioural Geography
  • Historical Geography
  • Cultural Geography
⚠ Exam Trap
Founder of Physical Geography = Polybius, while founder of Human Geography = Friedrich Ratzel — these founder names are commonly interchanged in exams.
🧭 Philosophies of Geography

1. Determinism

Human is subordinate to nature

Proponents:
  • Ratzel
  • Huntington
  • Ellen Semple
  • Ritter

2. Possibilism

Human capabilities take precedence over nature

Proponents:
  • Paul Vidal de la Blache
  • Lucien Febvre

3. Neo-Determinism

Equal importance to both human and nature

Originator:
  • Griffith Taylor
⚡ Quick Recall
Determinism → Nature dominates · Possibilism → Human dominates · Neo-Determinism → Stop & Go (Balance of both)
