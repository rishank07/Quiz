🌍 Earthquake
  • Vibrations in the Earth's crust • Seismic or elastic waves generated
  • Study of seismic waves → Seismology
  • Focus: Origin point of earthquake
  • Epicentre: Point on the surface directly above the focus
  • Isoseismal line: Connects areas of equal seismic intensity
📌 Earthquake Warning Signs Increased Radon gas in atmosphere • Animal behaviour changes (e.g. dogs)
〜 Types of Earthquake Waves

Primary Waves (P-waves)

  • Longitudinal / Sound / Compressional waves
  • Reach the surface first
  • Highest speed (8–13 km/s)
  • Travel through solid, liquid and gas
  • Capable of passing through the central part of Earth
  • Fill and Push

Secondary Waves (S-waves)

  • Transverse / Perpendicular waves
  • Average speed (4–6 km/s)
  • Travel only through solids
  • Slower than primary waves
  • Less destructive
  • Reach the seismograph after primary waves

Surface Waves (L-waves)

  • Love / Rayleigh (L / R-waves)
  • Restricted to the surface
  • Slowest speed (1–3 km/s)
  • Most destructive
  • Final waves to reach the surface and seismograph
  • Impact on water and land
⚠ Exam Trap
P-waves are the fastest but least destructive; Surface (L) waves are the slowest but most destructive — speed and destructiveness are inversely related here.

  • Region where no seismic waves are recorded
  • P-wave shadow zone – Between 105° to 145°
  • S-wave shadow zone – Beyond 105°
⚡ Quick Recall
Richter Scale = Logarithmic, measures Magnitude · Mercalli Scale = measures perceived Intensity/shaking
🏗️ Types of Earthquake
  • 1. Tectonic Earthquake – Due to collision and slipping of plates
  • 2. Collapse Earthquake – Underground collapse of mine or cave
  • 3. Volcanic Earthquake – Due to volcanoes
  • 4. Reservoir Induced Earthquake – Due to dam construction
  • 5. Explosion Earthquake – Due to nuclear tests or bomb explosion
💥 Effects of Earthquake
  • Tsunami occurrence
  • Landslides / Avalanches
  • Floods / Forest fires
  • Soil liquefaction

Zone-5 covers the smallest area (11%) but is the most seismically active; Zone-2 covers the largest area (41%) but has the lowest intensity — area share and risk level are inversely related.
🌊 Tsunami (Japanese word)
  • Tsu: Harbour • Nami: Wave — "Harbour Wave"
  • Waves generated due to undersea earthquakes or volcanic eruptions
  • Devastating Tsunami of Indian Ocean: 26 December, 2004
⚡ Quick Recall
Tsunami = "Harbour Wave" (Japanese) → triggered mainly by undersea tectonic earthquakes.
🌋 Volcano — "Safety Valve of Nature"
  • Natural vent or fissure through which molten substances of Earth like lava, ash, steam, etc. emerge
  • Materials emitted: Gases and water vapour (60–90%), Tuff (rock fragments), Lapilli (pea-sized particles), Breccia, Lava, Pyroclasts (solid rock fragments), etc.
📌 Magma vs Lava Magma (inside crust) → becomes Lava on surface

Constant release of dust, smoke, vapour, etc.

  • Kilauea (Hawaiian Islands) — Most active
  • Mt. Etna (Italy)

🏔️ Volcanic Landforms

  • Dyke, Sill, Laccolith, Phacolith, Lapolith, Batholith

External Landforms

⚠ Exam Trap
Dormant ≠ Extinct: Dormant volcanoes (e.g. Vesuvius, Fujiyama) can still erupt again; Extinct volcanoes (e.g. Kilimanjaro) have no eruption potential — Krakatoa is famously "dormant," not extinct, despite long quiet periods.

🧩 Plate Tectonic Theory
  • Study related to behaviour and movement of plates
  • Earth's rigid lithosphere is broken into Plates
  • Theory proposed by: Harry Hess (1962)
  • Scientific/mathematical explanation: W.J. Morgan
  • Other key contributors: McKenzie, Parker, Holmes
  • Driven by heat generated from radioactive substances inside the Earth
📌 Mechanism Movement of the lithosphere over the asthenosphere
🔀 Plate Boundaries
  • Destructive Plate Boundary – Formation of Rockies, Andes, Himalayas
  • Constructive Plate Boundary – Formation of new crust
  • Conservative Plate Boundary – Formation of transform faults (e.g. San Andreas Fault, California)
📜 Other Important Theories
  • Precursor Hypothesis: F.B. Taylor (early continental displacement idea)
  • Continental Drift Theory: Alfred Wegener (German Scientist) – 1912, based on gravitational force, Guy-Sam-Fit fit of coastlines, buoyancy, and tidal force
  • Pangea – the original supercontinent
  • Angara Land – North America, Europe, Asia
  • Gondwana Land – South America, Africa, India, Madagascar, Australia
⚠ Exam Trap
The widely credited Continental Drift Theory belongs to Alfred Wegener (1912) — F.B. Taylor's idea was an earlier, separate precursor concept, not the main "Continental Drift Theory" itself.
