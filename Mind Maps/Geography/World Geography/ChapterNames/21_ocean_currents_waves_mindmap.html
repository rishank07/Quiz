🌊 Ocean Currents
  • Long-distance water flow in a fixed direction
  • Two types: Warm and Cold currents
  • Meeting region of cold + warm currents → Rich fishing area (development of plankton)
  • Cold current region → Contributes to formation of coastal deserts
🌡️ Warm Air vs Cold Air — Water-holding Capacity

Warm Air

Higher water-holding capacity

Cold Air

Lower water-holding capacity

⚡ Quick Recall
Wherever warm and cold currents meet, nutrient-rich upwelling creates some of the world's best fishing grounds (e.g. off Newfoundland, Japan).
🌀 Gyre
  • Rotating water flow in oceanic/marine regions (whirlpool-like circulation)
  • Part of the oceanic water cycle
  • Driven by global wind patterns, the Coriolis effect (Earth's rotation), and the presence of continents
🧭 Causes of Origin of Ocean Currents

Physical Causes

  • Earth's rotational speed
  • Difference in density
  • Difference in ocean salinity

Atmospheric Causes

  • Temperature difference
  • Air pressure and winds

Other Causes

  • Shape of coastline
⚡ Quick Recall
Gyres form due to the combined effect of wind patterns + Coriolis force + continental barriers — no single factor alone explains them.

  • North Pacific Current Warm

  • Oyashio Current Cold
  • Aleutian Current Warm

South Pacific Ocean Currents

  • South Equatorial Current Warm
  • East Australian Current Warm
  • Peru / Humboldt Current Cold
  • El-Niño Current Warm
  • La Niña Cold
  • Counter Equatorial Current Warm
⚠ Exam Trap
El-Niño (warm phase) and La Niña (cold phase) are opposite ends of the same Pacific climate cycle (ENSO) — not two unrelated currents.

⚡ Quick Recall
Gulf Stream (warm, US East Coast → Europe) and Labrador Current (cold, Canada) are the two most exam-favourite Atlantic currents — they meet near Newfoundland's famous fishing grounds.
🌊 Indian Ocean Currents

North Indian Ocean (Monsoon-driven, reverses seasonally)

  • Summer: North Equatorial Current, South-west Monsoon Current, Counter Equatorial Current Warm
  • Winter: North-East Monsoon Current, Counter Equatorial Current Warm

South Indian Ocean

  • South Equatorial Current Warm
  • Mozambique Current Warm
  • Agulhas Current Warm
  • Madagascar Current Warm
  • West Wind Drift Cold
  • West Australian Current Cold
⚠ Exam Trap
Unlike the Pacific and Atlantic, the North Indian Ocean's currents completely reverse direction between summer and winter, driven by the monsoon winds — a uniquely Indian-Ocean phenomenon.
🌦️ Effects of Ocean Currents
EffectDetails
RainfallWarm currents bring rain • Example: Summer rainfall in China Type Climate regions • Warm currents flow parallel to east coasts of continents
Tropical CyclonesWarm currents help in the formation of tropical cyclones
Desert FormationCold currents flow parallel to west coasts → cause aridity and desert formation
Effect on NavigationCurrents assist or hinder ship navigation • Icebergs carried by the Labrador Current affect shipping routes
FishingMeeting of cold and warm currents → development of plankton → rich fishing grounds
⚡ Quick Recall
Warm currents → east coasts, more rain, cyclone-friendly · Cold currents → west coasts, aridity, coastal deserts.
🌊 Oceanic Waves
  • Upper part of a wave is called the Crest; lower part is called the Trough
  • Longshore Current: Flows parallel to the coast
  • Tsunami Waves: Extremely destructive – caused by undersea earthquakes/volcanic eruptions
