🌍 Rotation — Earth's Daily Motion
  • Earth's rotation on its axis → Daily Motion
Key PointDetails
Rotation Period23 hours 56 minutes 6 seconds (Sidereal Day)
DirectionWest to East
Rotation SpeedMaximum at Equator • Minimum at poles
Tilt of Earth's Axis23½°
Inclination of Earth's axis relative to orbital plane66½°
Speed at Equator1670 km/hr
⚡ Effects of Rotation
  • Occurrence of Day and Night
  • Change in direction of winds and ocean currents
  • Tides in oceans
⚡ Quick Recall
Axis Tilt = 23½° · Inclination to orbital plane = 66½° → (90° − 23½° = 66½°)
🪐 Revolution — Earth's Annual Motion
  • Along with rotation, Earth's movement around the Sun → Annual Motion
Key PointDetails
Revolution Period365 days 6 hours 9 minutes 9 seconds
Path of RevolutionAnticlockwise, Elliptical path
EffectsChange in seasons • 6-month day and 6-month night at poles • Determination of year duration
📅 Leap Year
  • At an interval of 4 years
  • Number of days in a year – 366 days
  • February month – 29 days
  • Due to the tilt of Earth's axis, days and nights become longer or shorter
📏 Distance between Sun and Earth
PositionDetails
Minimum – Perihelion3 January → 147 million km
Maximum – Aphelion4 July → 152 million km
📌 Apside Line Line joining Perihelion and Aphelion
⚠ Exam Trap
Perihelion (nearest, 3 Jan) ≠ Aphelion (farthest, 4 July) — counter-intuitively Earth is closest to the Sun during Northern Hemisphere winter.
☀️ Solstice (Sankranti / Ayanant)
  • Limits of Sun's Uttarayan (northward) and Dakshinayan (southward) movement
  • Point of maximum difference in day-night duration

Summer Solstice

Also known as Cancer Sankranti
  • Sun's rays fall vertically on Tropic of Cancer
  • Longest day in Northern Hemisphere (21 June)
  • Higher temperature at North Pole
  • 6-month long day begins at North Pole

Winter Solstice

Also known as Capricorn Sankranti
  • Sun's rays fall vertically on Tropic of Capricorn (22 December)
  • Longest day in Southern Hemisphere
  • 6-month long day begins at South Pole
  • Maximum temperature at South Pole
📌 Important Facts
  • Midnight Sun in Norway – 21 June
  • Shortest day in Northern Hemisphere – 22 December
  • Summer Solstice in Southern Hemisphere – December
  • 21 March and 23 September – Equal day and night globally
⚡ Quick Recall
Summer Solstice = 21 June (N. Hemisphere longest day) · Winter Solstice = 22 December (N. Hemisphere shortest day)
⚖️ Equinox
  • Sun's vertical rays fall on the Equator
  • Equal day and night everywhere
  • No pole tilting towards the Sun

Vernal Equinox – 21 March

  • Northern Hemisphere – Spring
  • Southern Hemisphere – Autumn

Autumnal Equinox – 23 September

  • Northern Hemisphere – Autumn
  • Southern Hemisphere – Spring
⚠ Exam Trap
Vernal Equinox brings Spring to the Northern Hemisphere but Autumn to the Southern — the season label flips depending on hemisphere.
🌑 Solar Eclipse (Surya Grahan)
  • Condition: Moon comes between Sun and Earth (Conjunction position)
  • Time: New Moon (Amavasya)
🔆 Types of Solar Eclipse

Total Solar Eclipse

Full Sun covered

Partial Solar Eclipse

Partial Sun covered

Annular Solar Eclipse

Ring of Sun visible around Moon

✨ Important Events During Solar Eclipse
  • Corona of the Sun (Ultraviolet rays) visible
  • Diamond Ring phenomenon
  • Dark Shadow: Umbra
  • Light Shadow: Penumbra
⚡ Quick Recall
Umbra = Dark/Complete shadow · Penumbra = Light/Partial shadow
🌕 Lunar Eclipse (Chandra Grahan)
  • Condition: Earth comes between Sun and Moon (Opposition position)
  • Time: Full Moon (not on every full moon)
  • Types: 1) Partial   2) Complete
  • Difference in Axis of Moon and Earth – 5°
🌙 Main Moon Phases

Super Moon

Moon closest to Earth

Blue Moon

Two full moons in one month

Blood Moon

Tetrad

📌 Important Moon Facts
  • Sun, Moon, and Earth in one line – Syzygy
  • Moon's nearest distance from Earth: Perigee
  • Moon's farthest distance from Earth: Apogee
  • Maximum 7 lunar and solar eclipses possible in a year
⚠ Exam Trap
Perigee (nearest) and Apogee (farthest) are often swapped with Perihelion/Aphelion (Sun-Earth distances) — Perigee/Apogee apply to the Moon, Perihelion/Aphelion apply to the Sun.
🌊 Tides
  • Rise of sea water (Tide) and fall of sea water (Ebb)
Cause of Occurrence
  • Gravitational pull of the Sun
  • Gravitational pull of the Moon
  • Centrifugal force of Earth
Theory Progressive Wave Theory (William Whewell)
🌗 Types of Tides

Spring Tide (High Tide)

  • Sun, Earth, and Moon in a straight line
  • Time – Full Moon and New Moon

Neap Tide (Low Tide)

  • Sun, Earth and Moon at right angles
  • Time – 7th and 8th day of Krishna and Shukla Paksha
📌 Important Tide Facts
  • Time gap between two tides – 12 hours 26 minutes
  • World's highest tide: Bay of Fundy → Nova Scotia (Canada)
  • Generally 7-day gap between Spring and Neap tides
  • Tidal Energy (in coastal areas), helpful in fisheries
⚡ Quick Recall
Spring Tide → Sun+Moon aligned (Full/New Moon) · Neap Tide → Sun+Moon at right angles
