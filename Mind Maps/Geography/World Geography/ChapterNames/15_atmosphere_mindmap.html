🌦️ Troposphere
  • Height: 0–18 km (Pole: 8 km, Equator: 18 km, Average: 13 km)
  • Lowest layer of atmosphere
  • All weather phenomena occur here
  • Normal Lapse Rate of temperature: 1 km → 6.5°C decrease
  • Tropopause: boundary separating Troposphere and Stratosphere
✈️ Stratosphere
  • Up to 50 km
  • Increase in temperature with height
  • Temperature stable up to 20 km
  • Suitable for airplane flight
  • "Mother of Pearl" clouds present here
☄️ Mesosphere
  • Up to 80 km
  • Decrease in temperature with increasing height
  • Coldest layer of the atmosphere
  • Burning up of meteoroids occurs here
📡 Ionosphere / Thermosphere
  • 80–640 km
  • Increase in temperature with increasing height — hottest layer
  • Reflection of radio waves
  • Red glow of Aurora at poles
  • Northern Lights (Aurora Borealis) and Southern Lights (Aurora Australis)
🌌 Exosphere
  • From Thermosphere to about 10,000 km
  • Composed mainly of Hydrogen (H₂) and Helium (He) gases
📌 Karman Line (100 km) Line separating atmosphere from outer space
⚠ Exam Trap
Temperature decreases with height in Troposphere and Mesosphere, but increases with height in Stratosphere and Thermosphere — the pattern alternates layer by layer.
🛡️ Ozone Layer (15–35 km, in Stratosphere)
  • Protects Earth from the Sun's ultraviolet rays
  • Maximum concentration – 22 km height
  • Measurement unit – Dobson unit
  • Maximum depletion – Over South Pole (Antarctica)

🕳️ Ozone Depletion & Protection
  • Depletion – Thinning / Hole in the ozone layer
  • Montreal Protocol (Canada) → Aims to reduce CFCs (Chlorofluorocarbons)

⚡ Quick Recall
Montreal Protocol → targets CFCs · Kigali Amendment (an amendment to Montreal Protocol) → targets HFCs
☀️ Insolation
  • Solar radiation energy received from the Sun
  • Aphelion (4 July): Relatively receives less insolation
  • Perihelion (3 January): Relatively receives more insolation
  • Equator – comparatively less • Deserts – maximum
🧭 Factors Affecting Insolation
  • Latitude
  • Conditions of the atmosphere
  • Day length
  • Transparency of atmosphere
  • Sunspots
  • Distance between Earth and Sun
⚠ Exam Trap
Deserts receive more insolation than the Equator, despite the Equator getting more direct sunlight overall — because deserts have clear skies and low humidity/cloud cover, allowing more solar radiation to reach the surface.
⚖️ Heat Budget
  • Balance of temperature (Earth not getting excessively hot or cold)
  • Average temperature of Earth – 15°C
  • Solar radiation / amount of energy received by Earth's surface – ~51%
🪞 Albedo
  • Amount of light reflected from any surface or object
  • Earth's average surface albedo value – 23% to 34%
  • Maximum Albedo – Ice / Glacier (40–70%)
  • Albedo of Dry sand – 35–45%
⚡ Quick Recall
Higher Albedo = More reflection, less absorption → Ice/Snow (highest albedo) stays cooler.
🔥 Heating and Cooling of the Atmosphere
ProcessKey Details
ConductionTransfer of heat from higher to lower temperature • Heating of lower layers of atmosphere
ConvectionVertical transfer of heat • Vertical heating of atmosphere
RadiationHeating through transmission of heat waves • Earth's heating from solar radiation
AdvectionHorizontal transfer of heat • 'Loo' is the result of advection
⚠ Exam Trap
Convection = vertical heat transfer; Advection = horizontal heat transfer — the hot "Loo" winds of North India are a classic example of Advection, not Convection.
🌡️ Temperature Variation
  • Causes: Latitude, Distance from sea, Ocean Currents, Altitude, Air circulation, Local factors
  • Isotherms: Lines connecting places having equal temperature
  • Temperature Range: Difference between maximum and minimum temperature
🔄 Temperature Inversion
  • Lower layer of air is cold and upper layer is warm (reversal of the normal pattern)

Favourable Conditions

  • Dry and Calm air
  • Long cold nights
  • Cloudless and clear sky
  • Stability in atmosphere

Effects

  • Formation of toxic fog / smog in industrial areas
⚡ Quick Recall
Temperature Inversion is exactly the opposite of the normal Lapse Rate (normally temperature falls with height in the Troposphere).
