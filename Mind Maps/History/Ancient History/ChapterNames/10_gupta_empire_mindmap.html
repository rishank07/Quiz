Exam Trap #1 — Founder vs Real Founder

Shri Gupta ne dynasty start ki, lekin Chandragupta I ko "Real / Actual Founder" mana jata hai. Exam mein "founder" ka seedha matlab Shri Gupta, "actual/real founder" = Chandragupta I.

1. Shri Gupta 240–280 CE
Adiraj · Maharaj
  • Poona Copperplate Inscription of Prabhavati Gupta mein 'Adhiraj' title mentioned
  • Magadha mein Chinese Buddhist Pilgrims ke liye temple construct kiya
  • Temple ke liye 40 villages ka revenue donate kiya
  • Chinese traveler I-tsing ne is temple ka mention kiya
2. Chandragupta I 319–335 CE
Maharajadhiraj · Real Founder
  • Dynasty ka first famous ruler
  • Real Founder of Gupta Dynasty
  • Licchhavi princess Kumaradevi se marriage
3. Samudragupta 335–380 CE
Parakramanka · Kaviraja · India's Napoleon
  • Coins par Veena bajate hue depict — Music Lover
  • Nickname: Licchhavi Dauhitra (Grandson of Licchhavis)
  • Court Poet Harishena ne Prayag Prashasti (Allahabad Pillar Inscription) compose kiya — eulogistic inscription
  • Historian V.A. Smith ne "India's Napoleon" kaha kyunki kabhi defeat nahi hua
5 Groups of Conquered Regions
# Region
1st Rulers of Ganga-Yamuna Doab
2nd Eastern Himalaya — Nepal, Assam, Bengal
3rd Atavika states (Vindhyan Region, forest areas)
4th Eastern Deccan + 12 South rulers (released after defeat; reached Kanchi, TN)
5th Shaka & Kushana rulers (some in Afghanistan)
4. Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya) 380–412 CE
Peak of Gupta Empire
  • Brother Ramagupta ki wife Dhruvadevi se marriage
  • Daughter Prabhavati ka marriage — Vakataka ruler Rudrasena II se (Brahmin rulers of Central India)
  • Western Malwa aur Gujarat par supremacy; Shaka Kshatrapa rule khatam kiya
  • Pehla Gupta ruler jisne Silver Coins introduce kiye
  • Second capital: Ujjain
  • Mehrauli Iron Pillar — king "Chandra" ka mention = Chandragupta II identified
  • Chinese traveler Faxian (399–414 CE) aaya — China se Bengal journey
  • 409 CE: Shaka ruler Rudrasimha III defeated — Malwa region mein tiger-style silver coins jaari
  • First silver coin circulation — Devashri written on coins

5. Kumaragupta I (Mahendraditya) 415–455 CE
Nalanda Founder
  • Coins par Lakshmi aur Ganga ki depictions
  • Ashwamedha Yagya perform kiya + Ashwamedha-type coins
  • Founded Nalanda University — Mahayana Buddhism ka Oxford; Ghatika Yantra (water clock) use
  • Pushyamitra tribe ka invasion (mention in Bhitari Pillar inscription of Skandagupta) — defeated
  • Sabse zyada Gupta period inscriptions: 18 received
  • Largest hoard of gold coins: Bayana coin hoard (Rajasthan) — 623 coins
  • Most coins in Peacock style (kuch silver bhi — Madhya Pradesh)
6. Skandagupta (Shakraditya) 455–467 CE
Hun Repeller
  • Title Shakraditya — Kahaum stone inscription se pata chala
  • Huns invasion repelled (mention in Bhitari Inscription)
  • Saurashtra province governor: Parnadatta (Junagarh inscription)
  • Sudarshan Lake dam reconstruction — Girnar admin Chakrapalita dwara
  • China ke saath friendly relations — ambassador bheja
  • Successor: Bhanugupta — Eastern Malwa mein Huna rule ka end
  • Eran Inscription — First epigraphic evidence of Sati system
Administrative System
  • Kingship — Hereditary
  • Permanent army
  • Villagers se Visti (Free/Forced Service) — officers aur army ke liye
  • Decentralization ki badhti pravritti
  • Bhoga — king ko fruits aur flowers ki periodic supply
  • Senabhakti — army pass hone par villagers dwara food supply
Taxes
  • Land taxes ka number badha
  • State share of produce: 1/6 to 1/4 (Bhaga)
  • Commercial taxes kam
  • Uparikara — additional tax
Social & Religious Status
👥 Social
  • Patriarchal society
  • Varna divided into sub-castes
  • Women — share in land rights (per Katyayana)
  • Village grants on large scale
🙏 Religious
  • Idol worship common
  • Buddhism ko royal patronage band
  • Supreme deity: Vishnu (Narayana)
  • Vaishnava religion most influential

⚠️ Exam Traps — Gupta Empire
Trap #1 — Founder vs Actual Founder

Shri Gupta = Founder (275 CE) | Chandragupta I = Actual/Real Founder (319 CE). Dono alag hain — confuse mat karo.

Trap #2 — India's Napoleon

Samudragupta ko "India's Napoleon" kaha — historian V.A. Smith ne. Napoleon ki tarah kabhi defeat nahi hua.

Trap #3 — First Silver Coins

Chandragupta II pehla Gupta ruler tha jisne Silver Coins introduce kiye. Samudragupta ya Chandragupta I nahi.

Trap #4 — Chinese Travelers

Faxian (Fa Hien) aaya Chandragupta II ke time (399–414 CE). I-tsing ne Shri Gupta ke time ka temple mention kiya. Dono different travelers hain, different periods.

Trap #5 — Nalanda University

Kumaragupta I ne Nalanda University ki foundation rakhi — Chandragupta II ya Samudragupta ne nahi. Nalanda = Oxford of Mahayana Buddhism.

Trap #6 — Sati Pratha — First Evidence

Sati pratha ka first epigraphic evidence = Eran Inscription (Skandagupta era). Bhitari Inscription nahi.

Trap #7 — Bayana Coin Hoard

Largest gold coin hoard = Bayana, Rajasthan — 623 coins. Yeh Kumaragupta I ke time se related hai. Exam mein "largest hoard" = Bayana.

Trap #8 — Prayag Prashasti Author

Allahabad Pillar Inscription = Prayag Prashasti. Yeh Harishena (Samudragupta's court poet) ne compose ki. Kalidas ne nahi.
