Chandragupta Maurya (322–298 BCE)
Founder of Mauryan Empire
  • Establishment: Defeated Dhana Nanda · Occupied Pataliputra 322 BCE with Chanakya's help
  • Greek Identity: Identified as Sandrokottos by British scholar William Jones
  • Army: Pan-Indian Empire · Army of 6,00,000 (Greek writer Justin)

Later Life

  • Migrated to Shravanabelagola · Performed Sallekhana
  • Play Mudrarakshasa (by Vishakhadatta) describes his rise
Bindusara (298–272 BCE)
Son of Chandragupta
  • Greek Title: Amitrochates / Amitraghata — "Slayer of enemies"
  • Religion: Patron of Ajivika sect
  • Administration: Council of 500 ministers · Chief Minister: Khalatanka
  • Revolts in Takshashila (Divyavadana): 1st revolt → sent Ashoka · 2nd revolt → sent Sushima
Foreign Ambassadors in Bindusara's Court

Ashoka (273–232 BCE)
Greatest Mauryan ruler
  • Accession: 273 BCE · Coronation: 269 BCE (4-year struggle among brothers)
  • First Indian king to directly address public through inscriptions
  • Standard Script: Brahmi · Standard Language: Prakrit
  • Kalinga War: 261 BCE (8 years after coronation)
  • Inscriptions read by: James Prinsep
  • Compiled by: Alexander Cunningham (published 1877)

⚔ Kalinga War (261 BCE) & Dhamma Policy ▼
Year
261 BCE
8 years after coronation
Toll
1 lakh killed · 1.5 lakh prisoners
Result
Bherighosha → Dhammaghosha
War-drum replaced by Dhamma
  • Renounced physical conquest → Cultural conquest (Dhamma)
  • Sent son Mahendra and daughter Sanghamitra to Sri Lanka
  • Appointed Dhamma Mahamatras (special officers)
  • 13th Major Rock Edict: Mentions 5 Yavana (Greek) kings — sent Dhamma preachers
  • 3rd Buddhist Council: 250 BCE at Pataliputra · Chairman: Moggalliputta Tissa · Compiled Abhidhamma Pitaka

Key Economic Facts
  • Tax rate: 1/6th of produce
  • Arthashastra: 27 superintendents appointed to regulate economic activities
  • Port (West): Bharuch
  • Port (East): Tamralipti
Social Condition (per Megasthenes)
  • Indian Society: 7 classes — Philosophers, Farmers, Hunters & Cattle Herders, Artisans, Soldiers, Magistrates & Councillors
  • Women: Remarriage and Niyoga allowed
  • Noble women: Stayed at home — Kautilya called them Anishkasini
  • Megasthenes & Strabo: No slavery in India
  • Arthashastra: Mentions many Varnashankara (mixed caste) communities — Ambashtha, Nishada, Magadha, Suta, Vena etc.
⚠ TRAP 01 — Chandragupta's Greek Identity
Chandragupta = Sandrokottos (Greek name)
Identified by British scholar William Jones
Sandrokottos helped link Mauryan chronology to Greek records
⚠ TRAP 02 — Accession vs Coronation of Ashoka
Accession = 273 BCE (became de facto ruler)
Coronation = 269 BCE (official coronation — 4-year gap due to power struggle)
Exam mein dono alag-alag puchhe jaate hain!
⚠ TRAP 03 — Bindusara vs Ajivika
Bindusara = Patron of Ajivika sect
Greek name: Amitrochates / Amitraghata = "Slayer of enemies"
Chandragupta = Jain · Bindusara = Ajivika · Ashoka = Buddhist
⚠ TRAP 04 — 13th Rock Edict = Most Detailed
13th Major Rock Edict = Most detailed inscription
Describes: Kalinga War + Neighbouring states + Atavika tribes + Dhamma Diplomacy with Greek rulers
12th Edict = Sarva Dharma Samabhava (not 7th!)
⚠ TRAP 05 — Inscriptions Read By / Compiled By
James Prinsep = First read/deciphered Ashokan inscriptions
Alexander Cunningham = Published compilation in 1877
Dono alag hain — Prinsep = decipherer, Cunningham = compiler
⚠ TRAP 06 — Edict V vs Edict XII (Dhamma Mahamatras)
Edict V = Appointment of Dhamma Mahamatras
Edict XII = Women's welfare Mahamatras + Sarva Dharma Samabhava
Dono mein Mahamatras hain — number yaad rakho!
⚠ TRAP 07 — Silver vs Copper Coins
Silver coins = Karshapana, Dharana
Copper coins = Kakani, Ardhakakani
Royal Currency = Pana
Punch-marked coins = Silver (Peacock, hill, crescent moon)
⚠ TRAP 08 — Megasthenes: Slavery in India
Megasthenes & Strabo say: "No slavery in India"
But Kautilya's Arthashastra mentions many forms of dasa/slavery!
Exam mein "Megasthenes ke anusar" puchha jaaye → No slavery
⚠ TRAP 09 — City Administration (30-6-5)
Pataliputra city administration: 30 members → 6 Committees → 5 members each
Nagarika = Head of city (NOT Rajuka — Rajuka is District Officer)
