📖 Source — Rajatarangini ▼
  • Author: Kalhana · Year: 1160 CE
  • Style: Like Mahabharata (composition)
  • Kalhana was court poet of Harsh (Lohar dynasty, 1089–1101 CE)
  • Rajatarangini's description ends with Jayasimha (1128–1155 CE)
  • Muslim rule established in Kashmir: Shah Mir (1339 CE)
🏔 Karkota Dynasty · Founder: Durlabhavardhana (625 CE)
👑 Karkota Dynasty Rulers ▼
  • Chandrapida (713 CE): Faced Arab invasion
Lalitaditya Muktapida (724–760 CE)
Most famous Karkota ruler
  • Defeated Kambojas and Turks
  • Victories described in Rajatarangini
  • Constructed Martand Sun Temple
🌸 Utpal Dynasty · Founder: Avantivarman (855–883 CE)
👑 Utpal Dynasty Rulers ▼
  • Avantivarman: Established city of Avantipura · Officer Suya constructed canals
  • Sugandha: Famous female ruler
  • Queen Didda (980 CE): Established peace · Took tough decisions · Died 1003 CE
🏔 Lohar Dynasty · Founder: Sangramaraja (1003–1028 CE)
👑 Lohar Dynasty Rulers ▼
  • Harsh (1089–1101 CE): Kalhana was his dependent poet
  • Jayasimha (1128–1155 CE): Last ruler described in Rajatarangini
🌊 Pala Dynasty (750–1162 CE) · Founder: Gopala · Buddhist dynasty
👑 Pala Dynasty — Key Rulers Tripartite Struggle: Pala vs Pratihara vs Rashtrakuta ▼
Gopala (750–770 CE)
Founder · Buddhist · People's choice
  • Source: Khalimpur inscription of Dharmapala
  • People of Bengal made him ruler
  • Established Odantapuri (in Bihar)
Dharmapala (770–810 CE)
Most glorious Pala ruler · First in Tripartite Struggle
  • Titles: Samrat, Paramasaugata, Parameshwara, Lord of Uttarapatha, Vikramashila
  • Established Vikramashila University (Buddhist learning centre)
  • Defeated by Pratihara ruler Vatsaraja and Rashtrakuta ruler Dhruva
Devapala (810–850 CE)
Most powerful Pala ruler
  • Defeated Pratihara ruler Mihir Bhoja
  • Sailendra ruler Balaputradeva of Java → requested 5 villages for Buddhist monastery at Nalanda
  • Arab traveler Sulaiman referred to Pala empire as 'Ruhma'
Mahipala I (from 980 CE)
  • Attacked and defeated by Chola ruler Rajendra I during Bengal campaign
  • Last Ruler: Madanapal (1144–1162 CE) · During Rampala's time — rebellion by Kaivarta caste
🌸 Sena Dynasty · Founder: Samanta Sena (1070–1095 CE) · From Karnataka
👑 Sena Dynasty — Key Rulers ▼
Ballala Sena
Learned ruler · Title: Gaudeshwara
  • Composed: Danasagara and Adbhutasagara (on astronomy)
Lakshmana Sena
Last Hindu ruler of Bengal
  • Longest royal title in ancient India (Paramabhagavata, Arirajamardana Shankara)
  • Bhakhtiyar Khilji attacked 1202 CE · captured Nabadwip
  • Established victory pillars in Puri, Kashi and Prayag
  • Court poet: Jayadeva (title: Piyushavarsha)
  • Jayadeva composed: Gita Govinda, Prasannaraghava
  • Five jewels: Jayadeva, Dhoyi, Govardhan, Umapati, Sharana

🏹 Chauhan Dynasty of Shakambhari · Founder: Vasudeva · Sources: Harsha Stone Inscription (973 CE), Bijolia Stone Inscription (1169–70 CE)
👑 Chauhan Dynasty Rulers ▼
Vigraharaja IV / Bisaladeva (1150–64 CE)
  • Composed play Harikeli — parts engraved on walls of Adhai Din ka Jhopra
  • Expanded Chauhan territories · Captured Delhi from Tomaras
Prithviraj III (1178–1192 CE)
Greatest Chauhan ruler · Called Rai Pithora
  • Source: Prithviraj Raso — Author: Chand Bardai
  • Won First Battle of Tarain (1191 CE) against Muhammad Ghori
  • Lost and was killed in Second Battle of Tarain (1192 CE)
  • Jayachandra's (Gahadavala) daughter Sanyogita abducted by Prithviraj III

Ruler Key Facts

🌅 Paramara Dynasty of Malwa · Founder: Krishnaraja (Upendra) · Capital: Ujjain → Dhar
👑 Paramara Rulers ▼
Vakpati Munja (972–998 CE)
  • Built Munja Sagar Lake (in Dhar)
  • Victory over the Huns
  • Titles: Shrivallabha, Amoghavarsha, Prithvivallabha
Raja Bhoja (1010–1060 CE)
Most famous Paramara ruler
  • Titles: Navasahasanka, Navavikramaditya
  • Established Sanskrit university in Dharanagari
  • Constructed idol of Vagdevi (Saraswati)
  • Founded Bhojpur town
  • Books composed: Saraswati Kanthabharana, Siddhanta Sangraha, Vidyavinoda
⚠ TRAP 01 — Tarain Battles Result
First Battle of Tarain (1191 CE) = Prithviraj WON
Second Battle of Tarain (1192 CE) = Prithviraj LOST
Exam mein "kaun jeetaa?" seedha puchha jaata hai — year se identify karo!
⚠ TRAP 02 — Vikramashila vs Odantapuri Founder
Vikramashila University = Dharmapala (Pala)
Odantapuri = Gopala (Pala founder)
Dono Pala rulers hain — lekin alag-alag universities!
⚠ TRAP 03 — Rajatarangini Author & Patron
Rajatarangini = Written by Kalhana (1160 CE)
Kalhana was dependent poet of Harsh (Lohar dynasty, 1089–1101 CE)
Last ruler described = Jayasimha (1128–1155 CE)
⚠ TRAP 04 — Gita Govinda Author
Gita Govinda = Jayadeva (court poet of Lakshmana Sena)
Title: Piyushavarsha
Sena's "5 jewels": Jayadeva, Dhoyi, Govardhan, Umapati, Sharana
⚠ TRAP 05 — Dilwara Temple Builder
Dilwara Jain Temple (Mount Abu) = Bimal Shah
Bimal Shah was feudal lord of Chalukya (Solanki) ruler Bhima I
NOT built by the king directly — built by his feudal lord!
⚠ TRAP 06 — Tripartite Struggle
3 Participants: Pala + Pratihara + Rashtrakuta
First to enter = Dharmapala (Pala)
Dharmapala defeated by Pratihara Vatsaraja and Rashtrakuta Dhruva
Devapala defeated Pratihara ruler Mihir Bhoja
⚠ TRAP 07 — Martand vs Kailasanatha Temple
Martand Sun Temple = Lalitaditya Muktapida (Kashmir/Karkota)
Kailasanatha Temple = Narasimhavarman II (Pallava, Kanchipuram)
Dono famous temples hain — dynasty yaad rakho!
⚠ TRAP 08 — Raja Bhoja (Paramara) — NOT Bhoja Pala
Raja Bhoja (1010–1060 CE) = Paramara dynasty (Malwa)
Founded Bhojpur town · Sanskrit university at Dharanagari
Do NOT confuse with Bhoja (Pratihara) = Mihira Bhoja (Gurjara-Pratihara)!
