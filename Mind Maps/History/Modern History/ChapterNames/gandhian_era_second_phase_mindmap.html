Simon Commission (1927–28)
All-British Commission — Indians Excluded
📅
Announcement: 8 November 1927. Appointed by: Lord Birkenhead (Secretary of State for India).
👤
Chairman: Sir John Simon. Total Members: 7 — saare British.
🎯
Objective: Government of India Act 1919 ki working aur progress examine karna.
👥
Members: Clement Attlee, Harry Levy Lawson, Edward Cadogan, Vernon Hartshorn, George Lane Fox, Donald Howard.
Indians ka Reaction
🏛️
Congress Madras Session (27 Dec 1927): President M.A. Ansari. Decision: "Har stage par aur har tarike se" boycott.
📍
India Arrival: 3 February 1928, Mumbai. "Simon Go Back" naare lage.
Nehru Report (1928) — Indian Response
⚡
Challenge: Lord Birkenhead ne challenge diya — "Unified Constitutional Proposal banao."
👤
Committee Chairman: Motilal Nehru. Report Presented: August 1928.
👥
Key Members: Tej Bahadur Sapru, Subhash Chandra Bose, Ali Imam, Shuaib Qureshi, Mangal Singh, M.S. Aney, N.M. Joshi, G.R. Pradhan.
❌
Rejection: Muslim League (Jinnah, Shafi, Aga Khan) ne reject kiya → Jinnah ne apni "14 Points" rakhi.
7 all-British members = undemocratic Madras Session 1927 = M.A. Ansari (NOT Nehru) Nehru Report = Motilal Nehru (chairman) Rejection → Jinnah's 14 Points

Three Round Table Conferences — London
1st RTC
Date: 12 Nov 1930 – 19 Jan 1931
Presided: Ramsay MacDonald (British PM)
Inaugurated: George V
Reps: 90 total (58 BI + 16 PS + 16 British parties)
Congress: ABSENT
Demands: Muslim League + Ambedkar = Separate Electorate
Result: No consensus
2nd RTC
Date: 7 Sep – 1 Dec 1931
Secretary: Samuel Hoare
Viceroy: Lord Willingdon
Congress: Gandhi ji (sole rep)
Others: Sarojini Naidu, Annie Besant, Malaviya, Jinnah, Iqbal, Ambedkar
Result: Separate Electorate par no consensus
3rd RTC
Date: 17 Nov – 24 Dec 1932
Reps: Only 46
Congress: BOYCOTTED
Key Fact: Ambedkar = only person in all 3 RTCs
Result: White Paper (Mar 1933) → GOI Act 1935
Famous Quote — 2nd RTC
Winston Churchill (about Gandhi at 2nd RTC)
"Seditious Middle Temple lawyer now posing as a half-naked Fakir." (Deshdrohi vakeel jo ab adhnanga fakir ban gaya hai).
1st RTC = Congress absent 2nd RTC = Gandhi sole rep 3rd RTC = Congress boycotted Ambedkar = attended all 3 RTCs

Gandhi-Irwin Pact (Delhi Pact) — 5 March 1931
📅
Date: 5 March 1931. Also called: Delhi Pact.
👤
Principal Negotiator: Mahatma Gandhi. Intermediaries: Tej Bahadur Sapru, M.R. Jayakar, Shrinivas Shastri.
Terms of Agreement
# Term
1 Release all political prisoners (except violent offenders)
2 Return of confiscated property (zabt sampatti wapas)
3 Postponement of fine collection
4 Permission to make salt (coastal areas only)
5 Withdrawal of Emergency Ordinances
6 Congress agrees to participate in Round Table Conference

Communal Award & Poona Pact — 1932
Communal Award
📅
Announced: 16 August 1932. By: British PM Ramsay MacDonald.
📋
Main Feature: Separate Electorate for — Muslims, Sikhs, Indian Christians, Europeans, Anglo-Indians. Crucial: Extended to Harijans (Depressed Classes) bhi.
🍽️
Gandhi's reaction: Fast unto death (Aamran Anshan) — started 20 September 1932, Yerwada Jail, Pune.
Poona Pact — 24 September 1932
🤝
Between: Mahatma Gandhi ↔ Dr. B.R. Ambedkar.
✍️
Signatories: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar (on behalf of Depressed Classes) + Madan Mohan Malaviya (on behalf of Gandhi + Upper Caste Hindus).
📊
Result: (1) Separate Electorate for Depressed Classes abolished. (2) Reserved seats Provincial Legislatures: 71 → 148.
🏛️
Samata Sainik Dal (SSD): Established 1927 by B.R. Ambedkar — safeguard rights of depressed classes.
GOI Act 1935 — Path
📋
White Paper (Mar 1933) → Joint Select Committee (Chairman: Lord Linlithgow, formed by Samuel Hoare Dec 1934) → GOI Bill in British Parliament → GOI Act 1935 (3 August 1935).
Communal Award 16 Aug 1932 = Ramsay MacDonald Gandhi fast = 20 Sep 1932, Yerwada Jail Poona Pact 24 Sep 1932 = Ambedkar + Malaviya Reserved seats 71 → 148
