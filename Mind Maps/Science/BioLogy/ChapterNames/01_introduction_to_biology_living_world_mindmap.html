🔬 What is Biology?
  • Term 'Biology' coined by Lamarck and Treviranus
  • Father of Biology → Aristotle
  • Study of living organisms called Biology
  • Examples: Fungi, Algae, Bacteria, Plants, Animals, Humans, etc.
⚠ Exam Trap
Lamarck & Treviranus ne term coin kiya — Father of Biology Aristotle hai. Dono alag-alag facts hain, confuse mat karna!

🏷️ Taxonomy — Key Facts
  • Term 'Taxonomy' coined by → Augustin Pyramus de Candolle
  • Father of Taxonomy → Carolus Linnaeus
  • Taxonomy = Identifying, Naming & Classifying Living Organisms
⚠ Exam Trap
Taxonomy term coin kiya de Candolle ne — Father of Taxonomy Linnaeus hai! Dono alag hain.
📖 Binomial Nomenclature
  • Introduced by Carolus Linnaeus — book: Systema Naturae
  • Unique name = Genus + Species
  • Written in Italics; Genus starts with capital, species in small letters
Example:Homo sapiens → Genus: Homo | Species: sapiens
🔢 Taxonomic Hierarchy (Descending Order)
1Kingdom → 2Phylum (Animals) / Division (Plants) → 3Class → 4Order → 5Family → 6Genus → 7Species

🔢 Five Kingdom Classification — Overview
  • Proposed by Robert Whittaker in 1969
  • Criteria:
    • Cell Structure
    • Body Organisation
    • Mode of Nutrition
    • Reproduction & Phylogenetic relationship

Fungi ki cell wall Chitin se bani hoti hai — Cellulose nahi! Cellulose → Plants ka. Ye common MCQ trap hai.
⚡ Quick Recall
Sirf Monera = Prokaryotic (no true nucleus). Baaki sab Eukaryotic. Animalia ki cell wall absent.
🦠 Kingdom Monera
  • Absence of True Nucleus
  • Oldest known microorganisms on Earth
1. Archaebacteria (Ancient bacteria)
  • Methanogens — Methane producing, e.g. Methanobacterium
  • Halophiles — Saline marine, e.g. Halococcus
  • Thermoacidophiles — Hot sulphur springs, e.g. Sulfolobus
2. Eubacteria (True bacteria)
  • Cyanobacteria — Nitrogen fixing, blue-green autotrophic bacteria
  • Mycoplasma — No cell wall, parasitic or saprophytes
⚡ Quick Recall
Nostoc & Anabaena (Cyanobacteria) → Fix atmospheric nitrogen via specialized cells called Heterocysts
🌿 Symbiotic Organisms
  • Symbiotic relationship → Benefits to both organisms
🍄 Lichen
  • Fungi (water & mineral absorption) + Algae (photosynthesis)
  • Pioneer organism on bare rocks
  • Air pollution (SO₂) indicator
  • Source of Litmus dye
🌱 Mycorrhiza
  • Fungi (mineral absorption — Phosphorus, protection) + Roots of higher plant (shelter & nutrition)
  • Examples: Pinus and Orchids
⚠ Exam Trap
Lichen = Fungi + Algae. Pioneer on bare rocks. SO₂ indicator — MCQ mein aata hai. Litmus dye source bhi yaad rakho!
🔬 Ultramicroscopic Acellular Structures
Virus Viroids Prions
Discovery of first virus → Ivanowsky (TMV) Discovered by → T.O. Diener Discovered by → Stanley B. Prusiner
Crystallization → Stanley (TMV) Free, microscopic, circular particles of RNA Infectious protein particles
Obligate parasite Cause stunt disease in Chrysanthemum Cause Scrapie in sheep & goats
Connecting link between living & non-living — Cause CJD & Kuru disease in humans
Structure: Protein coat (capsid) + Nucleic acid (DNA or RNA) — —
⚡ Quick Recall
TMV = Tobacco Mosaic Virus. Ivanowsky = Discovery | Stanley = Crystallization. Virus = Living + Non-living dono features. Prions = Protein only (no nucleic acid).
⚠ Exam Trap
Viroids mein sirf RNA hota hai — koi protein coat nahi! Prions mein sirf protein hota hai — koi nucleic acid nahi! Virus mein dono hain.
