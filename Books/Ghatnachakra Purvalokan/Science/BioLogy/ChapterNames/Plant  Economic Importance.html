Pine, Fir, Spruce, Cedar, Larch and Cypress are the famous timber-yielding plants of which several also occur widely in the hilly regions of India.
All these belong to:
पाइन (Pine), देवदार (Fir), स्प्रूस (Spruce), सीडर (Cedar), लार्च (Larch) और सरू (Cypress) प्रसिद्ध लकड़ी देने वाले पौधे हैं जिनमें से कई भारत के पहाड़ी क्षेत्रों में भी व्यापक रूप से पाए जाते हैं।
ये सभी किसके अंतर्गत आते हैं:
✅ Correct Answer: (B)
  • Pine, Fir, Spruce, Cedar, Larch and Cypress are the famous timber-producing plants.
  • These are angular trees found in the mountain forests. All these belong to Clade gymnosperms.
  • The term gymnosperm literally means 'naked seeds', which is based on the unenclosed condition of their seeds (called ovules in their unfertilized state).
  • The non-encased condition of their seeds contrasts with the seeds and ovules of flowering plants (angiosperms), which are enclosed within an ovary.
  • पाइन, देवदार, स्प्रूस, सीडर, लार्च और सरू प्रसिद्ध लकड़ी उत्पादक पौधे हैं।
  • ये पहाड़ी जंगलों में पाए जाने वाले कोणीय (angular) पेड़ हैं। ये सभी क्लेड जिम्नोस्पर्म (Clade gymnosperms - अनावृतबीजी) के अंतर्गत आते हैं।
  • जिम्नोस्पर्म शब्द का शाब्दिक अर्थ 'नग्न बीज' (naked seeds) है, जो उनके बीजों की खुली अवस्था (अधूरी निषेचित अवस्था में बीजांड कहा जाता है) पर आधारित है।
  • उनके बीजों की यह खुली अवस्था फूलों वाले पौधों (एंजियोस्पर्म) के बीजों और बीजांडों के विपरीत है, जो एक अंडाशय (ovary) के भीतर बंद होते हैं।

उपरोक्त कथनों में से कितने सही हैं?
✅ Correct Answer: (D)
  • Mushrooms, which have always been appreciated for their culinary and nutritional value, are now increasingly valued for their many important medicinal properties, so much so that they are used not only as dietary food (functional foods) but also in the form of dietary supplements, nutraceuticals, and mycotherapy products. Medicinal mushrooms also represent a valuable natural source of biologically active compounds with fungicidal, herbicidal and insecticidal properties. Hence, statement 1 and 3 are correct.
  • Magic mushrooms, also known as psilocybin mushrooms or 'shrooms', have a long and rich history of use in various cultures worldwide for their psychoactive and spiritual properties. The active compounds in these mushrooms, psilocybin, and psilocin, can cause profound changes in perception, mood and thinking. These can induce hallucinogenic or psychedelic effects when consumed. Hence, statement 2 is correct.
  • Some mushrooms, such as Lions Mane, Ghost Fungus and Jack-O-Lantern, have bioluminescent properties, causing them to have a green glow. Certain mushrooms release a glow of cold light due to a chemical reaction between oxyluciferin molecules, an enzyme called luciferase, and oxygen. Hence, statement 4 is also correct.
